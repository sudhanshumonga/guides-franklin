Chapter 7, Question 7.52.5

7.52.5.Q—How should a government report restricted resources from an activity that benefits both the government and private parties? For example, a state government has a revenue sharing agreement through which it receives royalty revenue—80 percent of which is restricted to a particular purpose and 20 percent of which is to be held in trust for a third party. Should those resources be reported in two funds—a special revenue fund and a private-purpose trust fund?
A—Two separate funds may be used or a special revenue fund may be used. If a single special revenue fund is used, the resources held by the government for the "private purpose" should be reported as a liability. The remaining fund balance would be classified as restricted.
