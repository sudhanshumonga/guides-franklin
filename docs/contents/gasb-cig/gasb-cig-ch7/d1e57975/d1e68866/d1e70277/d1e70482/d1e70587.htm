Chapter 7, Question 7.72.5

7.72.5.Q—Can a public college or university reporting as a special-purpose government engaged only in business-type activities present its expenses using either natural or functional classifications?
A—Yes. Neither Statement 34 nor Statement 35 specifically requires a special-purpose government engaged only in business-type activities to report using either natural or functional classifications. It should be noted that paragraph 11b(7) of Statement 34 requires condensed financial information for the current and prior years to be presented as "program expenses, at a minimum by function" when included in MD&A. However, paragraph 138a of Statement 34 indicates that paragraphs 8–11, as amended, apply to special-purpose governments engaged only in business-type activities "as appropriate." Therefore, if an organization reports its expenses using natural classifications, the comparative information in MD&A should be presented in the same manner as that included in the basic financial statements.
