Chapter 7, Question 7.47.18

7.47.18.Q—How will the assets and liabilities of the internal service fund in Question 7.47.17 be reported in the statement of net position?
A—The capital assets and the long-term liabilities of the internal service fund constitute general capital assets and general long-term liabilities, respectively, and therefore should be reported in the governmental activities column. The "look-back" adjustment that results from eliminating the effects of the internal service fund's charges to business-type activities would increase or decrease the internal balances reported in the statement of net position. An example of this process is illustrated in Exercise 4 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3. Exercise 4 also includes an internal service fund that serves only enterprise funds. (See also Question 7.47.7 about carrying forward accumulated "look-back" adjustments.)
