Chapter Z, Question Z.49.9

Z.49.9.Q—Paragraph 20 of Statement 49 states that "an insurance recovery generally is realizable when the insurer admits or acknowledges coverage, potentially before covered outlays take place." When are recoveries from other responsible parties realizable?
A—Paragraph 87 in the Basis for Conclusions states that, "if the responsible parties to the remediation have negotiated their respective shares of the remediation outlays, and there is a reasonable belief that the parties are financially capable of paying their shares, the responsible parties have essentially ‘admitted or acknowledged coverage,' similar to an insurer," but that "if a responsible party or parties have not accepted responsibility or have not agreed to their share of the remediation efforts, it is analogous to an insurer's denying coverage." Thus, recoveries from other parties can be realizable when the parties have negotiated a cost-sharing agreement. However, paragraph 87 also states that "the realizability assessment may depend on a requirement that the government perform the remediation activities before recovery is considered realizable." That is, the cost-sharing agreement may require that remediation work first be performed before payment is required. Professional judgment is required to determine how a cost-sharing agreement affects the realizability of recoveries from other parties. Those recoveries should not be recognized as assets before they become realizable.
