FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS
•The City's total net position remained virtually unchanged over the course of this year's operations. However, while net position of our business-type activities increased $3.7 million (or 4.4 percent), this was offset by a decrease of $3.1 million (or 2.4 percent) in the net position of our governmental activities.
•During the year, the City's expenses were $6.4 million more than the $99.5 million generated in taxes and other revenues for governmental programs (before special items). This is better than last year, when expenses exceeded revenues by $8.9 million.
•In the City's business-type activities, revenues increased 9.2 percent to $15.5 million while expenses decreased 1.7 percent.
•The total cost of the City's programs was virtually unchanged (increasing approximately $900,000, or less than 1 percent), and no new programs were added this year.
•The general fund reported a decrease in fund balance this year of $1.3 million despite one-time proceeds of $3.5 million from the sale of some of our park land.
•The resources available for appropriation were $1.1 million less than budgeted for the general fund. However, we kept expenditures within spending limits primarily through a midyear hiring and overtime freeze and our staff restructuring efforts.
