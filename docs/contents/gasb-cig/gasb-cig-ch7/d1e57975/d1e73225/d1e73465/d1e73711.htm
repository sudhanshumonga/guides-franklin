Chapter 7, Question 7.104.9

7.104.9.Q—Could a government record capital assets, including infrastructure, in an amount equal to the balance of general long-term debt at the transition date?
A—Generally, no. Long-term debt is often issued to finance the purchase of capital assets, and the total amount of debt outstanding at a transition date may be a starting point for estimating the historical cost of those capital assets. The original issuance amount, rather than the balance outstanding at a transition date, may be a better estimate of original cost in certain cases.
