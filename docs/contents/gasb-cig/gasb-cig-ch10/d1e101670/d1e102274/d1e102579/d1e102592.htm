Chapter 10, Question 10.10.1

10.10.1.Q—In order to reduce its cost of borrowing, a government pays a premium to a counterparty for a credit default swap. The beneficiaries of the swap agreement are the city’s bondholders. How does the government report this agreement?
A—Even though the government made the premium payment, the credit default swap is an agreement between the counterparty (the writer of the credit default swap) and the government, with the city’s bondholders being the beneficiaries. Therefore, the government would not report the credit default swap in its financial statements. The government, however, should report premium payments as a cost of borrowing.
