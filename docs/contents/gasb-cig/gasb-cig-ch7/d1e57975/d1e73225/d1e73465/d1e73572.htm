Chapter 7, Question 7.104.5

7.104.5.Q—What provisions have been made to minimize the cost of reporting infrastructure assets?
A—Some of the provisions to minimize cost include:
•Several methods of estimating historical cost are permitted, including deflated current replacement cost and the use of bond documents and capital budgets (paragraphs 158 and 160 of Statement 34). See Exercise 8 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3.
•Accumulated depreciation at transition may be calculated using weighted-average acquisition years (paragraph 159 of Statement 34). See Exercise 9 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3.
•Although all governments are encouraged to report all infrastructure assets at the effective dates of the Statement, phase 1 and 2 governments (paragraph 143 of Statement 34) were permitted to defer retroactive reporting of infrastructure for four years. The smallest governments—phase 3 governments—are not required to capitalize infrastructure assets acquired prior to the implementation of Statement 34.
•Required capitalization is limited to major general infrastructure assets as defined by paragraph 148 of Statement 34 (footnote 66). See Question 7.106.1 and Exercise 10 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3.
•The required retroactive capitalization period need not extend back earlier than years ending after June 30, 1980 (paragraph 154 of Statement 34).
•Composite depreciation rates based on groupings of similar assets or classes of dissimilar assets are permitted (paragraph 163 of Statement 34). See Exercise 1 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3.
