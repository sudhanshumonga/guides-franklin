Chapter 9, Question 9.30.3

9.30.3.Q—A government's ability to borrow is limited both by a provision in the constitution of the state in which it is located and by a more restrictive statutory limit imposed by the state. Which limitation should be reported in the schedule of debt limitation information?
A—The more restrictive statutory limitation should be the basis of the schedule, because it is the effective limit above which the government may not borrow. It also may be useful to the reader of the schedule to include a note in the schedule that describes the nature of the constitutional limit as well.
