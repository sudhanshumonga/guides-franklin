Chapter 9, Question 9.20.2

9.20.2.Q—May a government voluntarily include rates imposed by its state government as overlapping rates?
A—Paragraph 18 of Statement 44 states that governments are not required to include overlapping state government rates, but they are not prohibited from doing so if they believe the information is meaningful. For instance, it might be valuable to the users of the financial report if a government reporting revenue capacity information for a sales tax included the sales tax rate imposed by the state government; the state rate is often as large as or larger than the rate imposed by the reporting government.
