Chapter 10, Question 10.43.1

10.43.1.Q—A government issues a 10-year zero coupon bond with a call option. The par rate for 10-year bonds is 5 percent. Does the omission of coupon payments make the bond an instrument with off-market terms resulting in an up-front payment as provided in paragraph 64c(1) of Statement 53?
A—No. The up-front payment arises from the sale of the zero-coupon bond, not the off-market terms of the call option. Paragraph 64c(1) applies if the embedded derivative instrument has off-market terms. If this bond had carried a 5 percent coupon, there would still have been an up-front payment. Hybrid instrument reporting, therefore, does not apply.
