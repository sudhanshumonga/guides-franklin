Chapter 7, Question 7.104.17

7.104.17.Q—A government that has adopted the requirements of Statement 34 in a prior year is retroactively reporting some of its general infrastructure in the current year. Is this reported as a current-year addition to general capital assets?
A—No. Retroactive reporting of infrastructure is presented as an adjustment of a prior period. Thus, beginning net position and any affected note disclosures, such as beginning balances in the schedule of changes in capital assets, are restated. Guidance that should be followed when comparative statements are presented is provided in paragraph 61 of Statement 62, as amended. (See Question 7.22.16 for additional discussion of that guidance.)
