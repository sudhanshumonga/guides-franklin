Chapter 10, Question 10.13.5

10.13.5.Q—Should transactions of derivative instruments that are exchange-traded be accounted for based on the trade date (the date the order to buy or sell the derivative instrument is placed) or the settlement date (the date that the cash and derivative instrument are exchanged)?
A—Transactions of derivative instruments that are exchange-traded (for example, a commodity future) should be accounted for based on the trade date. The trade date is the date on which the transaction occurred—the date the government acquires or sells ownership of the instrument.
