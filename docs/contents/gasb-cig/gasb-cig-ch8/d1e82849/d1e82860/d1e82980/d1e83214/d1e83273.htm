Chapter 8, Question 8.5.2

8.5.2.Q—In addition to retirement income and postemployment healthcare, the defined benefit pension plan in which an employer participates provides life insurance and long-term care benefits. Does Statement 45 require that those benefits, if offered through a defined benefit pension plan, be accounted for separately as OPEB?
A—No. Except for postemployment healthcare benefits (always OPEB), if other nonpension benefits are provided through a defined benefit pension plan, those benefits should be accounted for by the employer as pension benefits in conformity with Statement 68, as amended, or Statement 73, as applicable, and as a pension plan in conformity with Statement 67, as amended, if the plan is administered through a trust that meets the criteria in paragraph 3 of that Statement.
