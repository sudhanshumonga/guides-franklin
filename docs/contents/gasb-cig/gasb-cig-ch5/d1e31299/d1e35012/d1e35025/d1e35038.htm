Chapter 5, Question 5.116.1

5.116.1.Q—A single or agent employer provides pensions to its employees through a defined benefit pension plan that is administered through a trust that has the characteristics identified in paragraph 4 of Statement 68. The employer does not have a special funding situation (as defined by paragraph 15 of Statement 68) and does not have a payable to the pension plan. If there is no requirement that the employer make contributions to the plan, does Statement 68, as amended, apply to the employer?
A—Yes. If the single or agent employer provides benefits to its employees through a defined benefit pension plan that is administered through a trust (or equivalent arrangement) in which contributions to the pension plan from employers and nonemployer contributing entities and earnings on those contributions are irrevocable; pension plan assets are dedicated to providing pensions to plan members in accordance with the benefit terms; and pension plan assets are legally protected from the creditors of employers, nonemployer contributing entities, the pension plan administrator, and plan members, an employer that does not have a special funding situation should follow the provisions of paragraphs 18–47 of Statement 68, as amended, for pension liabilities to employees.
