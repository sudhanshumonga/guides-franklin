Chapter 6, Question 6.28.1

6.28.1.Q—Should investment transactions be accounted for based on the trade date (the date the order to buy or sell the investment is placed) or the settlement date (the date that the cash and investment instrument are exchanged)?
A—Investment transactions should be accounted for based on the trade date. The trade date is the date on which the transaction occurred and the date the government is exposed to (or released from) the rights and obligations of the ownership of the instrument. This guidance is consistent with paragraph 18 of Statement 67 and paragraph 24 of Statement No. 74, Financial Reporting for Postemployment Benefit Plans Other Than Pension Plans.
