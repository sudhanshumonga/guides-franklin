Chapter 6, Question 6.22.3

6.22.3.Q—The Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) prescribes a cost-based short-term investment fund for the banks that it regulates that is similar to a cost-based external investment pool described in paragraph 4 of Statement 79. Can the participants in the cost-based short-term investment fund prescribed by OCC value their investments using an amortized cost-based measurement?
A—No. The use of an amortized cost-based measurement as the basis for participants to value their investments is limited to investments in external investment pools that meet the criteria in paragraph 4 of Statement 79. A cost-based short-term investment fund that complies with the OCC requirements also would need to meet the criteria in paragraph 4 of Statement 79 for the fund’s participants to value their investments using an amortized cost-based measurement.
