Chapter 7, Question 7.8.7

7.8.7.Q—A government makes economic development loans to private parties at interest rates below commercially available lending rates. Statement 62, paragraph 180, generally prescribes discounting loans or notes receivable that earn interest at below-market rates. Is the government required to discount the loan at a higher imputed interest rate per Statement 62 and recognize additional interest income?
A—No. Because the government makes the low-interest loan to carry out governmental objectives, it “makes the market” for the loan. The market rate is the rate that the government charges.
