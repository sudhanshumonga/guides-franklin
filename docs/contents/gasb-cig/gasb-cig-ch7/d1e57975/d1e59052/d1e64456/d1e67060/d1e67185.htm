Chapter 7, Question 7.47.5

7.47.5.Q—A "lead" state agency obtains a federal grant and passes a portion through to another state agency. For the state's financial statements, would the lead agency consider the federal reimbursement (of the subrecipient agency's expenditures) that it passes through a reimbursement, or revenue and expense per Statement 24?
A—Because the portion provided to the other state agency is an internal transaction, there is no “pass-through” to invoke the requirements of Statement 24; therefore, the state function that includes the lead agency would not include revenue or expense from the grant. The federal reimbursement should be reported as program revenue of the function that includes the agency (the subrecipient) that reports the reimbursed expenses in the statement of activities. For parallel accounting in the governmental funds, the “passing through” from one agency to another should be accounted for consistent with a reimbursement. That is, the lead agency would reduce its revenue by the amount transferred to the subrecipient agency, rather than report the amount as revenue and a transfer.
