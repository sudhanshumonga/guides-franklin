Chapter 1, Question 1.37.2

1.37.2.Q—Suppose a government that is covered by a collateral pool cannot obtain the information it needs to determine whether its deposits are exposed to custodial credit risk—for example, because there is no state official with the ability or authority to act as a pool administrator. How would a preparer's inability to obtain information affect the required activity disclosures?
A—If a government cannot obtain the information necessary to determine the custodial credit risk of deposits covered by collateral pools, it should disclose that fact along with any important information regarding custodial credit risk that it is able to provide.
