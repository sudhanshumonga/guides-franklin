Chapter 1, Question 1.20.1

1.20.1.Q—Some investment securities are issued in paper form; that is, a certificate provides evidence of ownership. Sometimes security certificates are registered in the government's name. How does one determine if these investment securities are "held in the name of the government" and consequently not exposed to custodial credit risk if they are registered in the government's name and held by a custodian?
A—If investment securities are registered in the government's name and they have not been endorsed, they are not exposed to custodial credit risk. If the government has endorsed the registered securities, they should be evaluated as bearer securities or as street name or nominee name securities as discussed in Questions 1.21.1 and 1.22.1, respectively.
