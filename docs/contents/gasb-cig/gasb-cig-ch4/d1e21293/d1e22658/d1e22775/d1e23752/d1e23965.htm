Chapter 4, Question 4.22.5

4.22.5.Q—Would a primary government's guarantee (legal obligation) of a potential component unit's debt create a financial burden on the primary government even if it is highly improbable that the potential component unit will default on the debt?
A—Yes. The financial burden relationship is based on the potential for an organization to impose specific financial burdens on the primary government. If the primary government is legally obligated to assume responsibility for the debt of the potential component unit in the event of a default, a financial burden relationship exists between the primary government and the potential component unit.
