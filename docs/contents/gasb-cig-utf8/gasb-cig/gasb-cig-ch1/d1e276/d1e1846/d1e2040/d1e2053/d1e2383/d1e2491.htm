Chapter 1, Question 1.16.5

1.16.5.Q—Governments often use trust departments as trust or fiscal agents—for example, to hold bond reserves or amounts that shortly will be used to pay debt principal and interest. In these situations, the trust department both acquires and holds investment securities under the provisions of a trust agreement that details the trust department's fiduciary responsibilities. Are these "amounts held in trust" exposed to custodial credit risk?
A—Statement 40, as amended, requires custodial credit risk disclosures for investment securities when the counterparty is the custodian (see Questions 1.15.2 and 1.16.4) but does not address the particular situation of trust agreements. Professional judgment is necessary in light of the actual responsibilities of the trustee to determine whether the trustee is acting in the stead of the public funds treasurer or as the treasurer's counterparty.
