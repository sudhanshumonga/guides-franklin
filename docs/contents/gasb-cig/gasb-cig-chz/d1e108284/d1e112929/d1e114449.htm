Chapter Z, Question Z.54.46

Z.54.46.Q—If a governmental fund reports an inventory balance for items that will be sold, rather than used in providing services, should the fund balance pertaining to that inventory be classified as nonspendable?
A—Fund balance related to inventory should be classified as nonspendable, because the resources are not in a spendable form, unless the proceeds from the sales are restricted, committed, or assigned. Fund balance representing inventory balances held for sale should not be reclassified from the nonspendable category, if the proceeds from sales are expected to be unassigned.
