Chapter 1, Question 1.9.5

1.9.5.Q—Statement 40, as amended, requires that the credit quality ratings of NRSROs be disclosed, but it does not specifically identify to what detail the disclosures are required to be made. Ratings issued by the NRSROs can be quite detailed. For example, one of the NRSROs has applied numerical modifiers 1, 2, and 3 in each generic rating classification from Aa to B. The modifier 1 indicates that the issue ranks in the higher end of its generic rating category, the modifier 2 indicates a midrange ranking, and the modifier 3 indicates that the issue ranks in the lower end of its generic category. Although they do not use numerical modifiers, other NRSROs use pluses and minuses as modifiers. Should credit quality disclosures include such detail as numerical modifiers?
A—Credit quality modifiers are not a required disclosure.
