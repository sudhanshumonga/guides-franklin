Chapter Z, Question Z.60.2

Z.60.2.Q—In an agreement with a third party, a government establishes that the third party is required to charge prices that are within a reasonable range of those providing similar goods and services in the surrounding area. When evaluating whether this agreement is a service concession arrangement, would that provision constitute the ability of the government to modify or approve the prices or rates that can be charged for the services, as described in paragraph 4c of Statement 60?
A—Yes. Establishing an agreed-upon range of acceptable rates that a third party may charge is sufficient to meet the criterion regarding the ability to modify or approve the prices, as described in paragraph 4c of Statement 60.
