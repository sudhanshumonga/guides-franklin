Chapter 1, Question 1.24.2

1.24.2.Q—A government purchases investment securities from Bank A and uses an unaffiliated Bank Z for custody. Bank Z is the government's regular custodial agent and holds the investment securities in the government's name. However, Bank Z uses Bank A as its correspondent for settlement of all transactions and safekeeping of all securities. Bank A is holding the securities for Bank Z on behalf of Bank Z's customers. Are these investment securities subject to custodial credit risk disclosures?
A—No. The investment securities are not exposed to custodial credit risk because Bank Z is performing the custody function for the government despite its use of Bank A as a correspondent. The facts of the question show that Banks A and Z are unaffiliated and the government's use of Bank Z for custody and Bank Z's use of Bank A as a settlement and safekeeping agent are ordinary and regular business arrangements.
