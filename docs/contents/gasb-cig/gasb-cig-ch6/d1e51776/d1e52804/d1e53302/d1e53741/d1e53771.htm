Chapter 6, Question 6.22.2

6.22.2.Q—How does a government determine whether an external investment pool measures its investments at amortized cost in accordance with paragraph 4 of Statement 79?
A—The government should obtain this information from the pool sponsor. It also could review a recent financial statement of the pool. If the pool is a governmental entity, Statement 31, as amended, requires that it provide information in its financial statements that would permit governmental entities to determine whether it is measures all of its investments at amortized cost in accordance with paragraph 4 of Statement 79. Financial statements from nongovernmental pool sponsors also can provide information to verify a pool's representation about its status on whether it measures all of its investment at amortized cost in accordance with paragraph 4 of Statement 79.
