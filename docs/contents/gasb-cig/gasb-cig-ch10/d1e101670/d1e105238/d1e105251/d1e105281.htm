Chapter 10, Question 10.42.2

10.42.2.Q—A public university endowment holds a convertible bond as an investment. The conversion option, an embedded derivative instrument, permits the government to convert the bond into a stock investment. Should the convertible bond be reported as a hybrid instrument?
A—No. The convertible bond is an investment that is measured on the statement of net position at fair value. Hybrid instrument reporting, therefore, is not applicable (paragraph 64a of Statement 53, as amended).
