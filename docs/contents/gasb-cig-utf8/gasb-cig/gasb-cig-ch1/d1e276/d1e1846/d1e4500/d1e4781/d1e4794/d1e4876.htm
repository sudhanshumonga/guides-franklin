Chapter 1, Question 1.48.3

1.48.3.Q—In paragraph 29 of the Introduction and Background Information section, Statement 3 defines a mutual fund as an open-end investment company registered with the SEC. Some mutual funds are entities established under state law to serve local or special-purpose governments and have received a "no-action" letter from the SEC. Is a government's investment in such a fund subject to custodial credit risk disclosures?
A—No. Investments in mutual funds that are established by state law to serve local or special-purpose governments and that have received a "no-action" letter from the SEC are not exposed to custodial credit risk.
