Chapter Z, Question Z.69.1

Z.69.1.Q—A government dissolves and ceases to exist because all of its operations form the basis of a new government. No significant consideration is exchanged. Would this meet the definition of a transfer of operations?
A—Yes. Paragraph 10b of Statement 69, in defining a government merger, provides that the operations of the government entity that ceases to exist are absorbed into, and provided by, one or more continuing governments. Because the operations are continued by a newly formed government, this event would not meet the definition of a government merger. A transfer of operations, as defined in paragraph 12 of Statement 69, does not require that the transferor government continue to exist after the transfer of operations.
