Chapter 7, Question 7.59.2

7.59.2.Q—A governmental fund incurs a liability to the general fund with no specific repayment arrangement. Should the interfund payable be reported as a fund liability of that governmental fund under the modified accrual basis of accounting?
A—The government should either report the amount as a fund liability in accordance with Statement 34, paragraph 81, or reclassify it as a transfer, as discussed in paragraph 112a(1) of that Statement. Paragraph 81 states that liabilities arising from interfund activities do not constitute general long-term liabilities and therefore should be reported in governmental funds. Paragraph 112a(1) provides that, if repayment is not expected within a reasonable time, the interfund balances should be reduced and the amount that is not expected to be repaid should be reported as a transfer. (See Question 7.82.1 about the meaning of reasonable time.)
