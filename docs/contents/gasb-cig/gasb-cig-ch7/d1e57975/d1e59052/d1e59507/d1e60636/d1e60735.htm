Chapter 7, Question 7.13.4

7.13.4.Q—Are land improvements depreciable?
A—Improvements that produce permanent benefits—for example, fill and grading costs that ready land for the erection of structures and certain landscaping—are not depreciable. Alternatively, improvements that are considered part of a structure or that deteriorate with use or the passage of time, such as parking lots and fencing, should be considered depreciable. (See Question 7.9.1.)
