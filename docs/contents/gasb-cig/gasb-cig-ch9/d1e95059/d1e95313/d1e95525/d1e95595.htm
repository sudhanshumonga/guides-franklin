Chapter 9, Question 9.8.3

9.8.3.Q—If a government engaged in governmental or business-type activities has a pension or other postemployment benefit plan, is it required to present the information required for governments engaged only in fiduciary activities?
A—No. The information in paragraph 10b of Statement 44, as amended, is required to be presented only by governments that conduct only fiduciary activities, such as pension and other postemployment benefit plans, in their separately issued financial reports. However, a government employer may present this information voluntarily, particularly if its plan does not prepare a statistical section.
