Chapter 7, Question 7.47.19

7.47.19.Q—A state investment board is responsible for managing the investments of several state funds, including pension funds, internal service funds, enterprise funds, and governmental funds. The board is funded solely by service fees charged to these other funds and is reported as an internal service fund. A high percentage of the state investment board's activity pertains to the pension funds. Because fiduciary funds are not presented in the government-wide statements, should this internal service fund be included with governmental or business-type activities?
A—The balances of the internal service fund should be included with governmental activities in the government-wide statement of net position. The balances would be included with business-type activities only if enterprise funds are the predominant or only participants. The investments and related income, gains, and losses of the internal investment pool itself should be allocated to the participating funds and reported in accordance with paragraph 14 of Statement 31, as amended.
