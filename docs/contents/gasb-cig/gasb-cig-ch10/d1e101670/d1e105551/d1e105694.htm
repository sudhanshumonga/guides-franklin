Chapter 10, Question 10.46.6

10.46.6.Q—A government limits its derivative instrument counterparties to the two highest credit quality ratings. Is the government required to disclose dollar values for each rating, or may the disclosure indicate that all counterparties have the two highest ratings?
A—As defined in Statement 53, credit risk is the risk that a counterparty will not fulfill its obligations (paragraph 82). The magnitude of a government’s credit risk exposure is conveyed by disclosing the dollar value of each rating category. A disclosure indicating that the counterparties of all the derivative instruments entered into by the government have the two highest ratings as issued by NRSROs is not sufficient to clearly communicate the credit risk of those derivative instruments.
