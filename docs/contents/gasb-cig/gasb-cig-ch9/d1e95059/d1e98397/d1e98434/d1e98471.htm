Chapter 9, Question 9.36.2

9.36.2.Q—Should the government employees information be an average for the fiscal year, or should it reflect a particular point in time? If the latter, should the point be the last day of the fiscal year or another date?
A—The Statement does not specify the point at which the number of employees should be measured. Governments should use professional judgment to select a measure that best suits the needs of the user of their financial reports and should note the nature of that measure (in other words, fiscal-year average, as of fiscal year-end, and so on). Governments should use a consistent measure from year to year.
