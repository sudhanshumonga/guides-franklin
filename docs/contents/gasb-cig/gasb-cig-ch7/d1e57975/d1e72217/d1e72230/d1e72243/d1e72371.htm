Chapter 7, Question 7.91.5

7.91.5.Q—A government presents its budgetary comparison in a schedule as RSI. Can a budget-to-actual comparison for a nonmajor special revenue fund be included in that schedule?
A—No. By definition, the information presented in RSI is limited to that which is required. Paragraph 130 of Statement 34, as amended, sets the scope of the budgetary comparison as the general fund and major special revenue funds. Therefore, because the budgetary comparison schedule can only include major special revenue funds that have legally adopted budgets, any special revenue fund presented is a major fund, regardless of whether it otherwise would be required to be reported as a major fund pursuant to the percentage criteria in paragraph 76, as amended. Thus, the consequence of presenting additional special revenue funds in the budgetary comparison schedule is that those funds are subject to all major fund reporting requirements and are, therefore, not considered nonmajor.
