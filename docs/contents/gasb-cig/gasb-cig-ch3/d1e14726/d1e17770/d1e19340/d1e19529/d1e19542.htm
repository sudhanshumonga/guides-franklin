Chapter 3, Question 3.78.1

3.78.1.Q—When disclosing significant subsequent events, is pro forma financial information required?
A—No. Paragraph 80 of Statement 10, as amended, requires disclosure about events that occur after the statement of net position/balance sheet date where there is a reasonable possibility that an asset has been impaired or a liability incurred and where the financial statements may be misleading without this information. The required disclosures are (a) indications of the nature of the loss or loss contingency and an estimate of the amount or range of loss or possible loss or (b) a statement that an estimate cannot be made. Paragraph 80 only suggests that pro forma information may be the best way to make these disclosures, but it does not mandate that information.
