Chapter 7, Question 7.91.9

7.91.9.Q—What format should governments use for presentation of the budgetary comparison?
A—Paragraph 131 of Statement 34, as amended, states that governments may use the format, terminology, and classifications used in the underlying budget documents or those used in the governmental funds' statement of revenues, expenditures, and changes in fund balances. Both formats are allowed, and neither is preferred over the other. Examples using a budget document format are presented in Exhibits 12 and 13 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-1, and an example of the governmental statement of revenues, expenditures, and changes in fund balances format is presented in Exhibit 13 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-2.
