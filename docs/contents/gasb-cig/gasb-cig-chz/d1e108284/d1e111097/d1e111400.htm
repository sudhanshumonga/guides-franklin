Chapter Z, Question Z.49.4

Z.49.4.Q—Instead of separately estimating ranges of possible outcomes and associated probabilities for the various parts of a pollution remediation obligation, may governments use average costs developed by other parties?
A—Yes, in certain circumstances. For example, a local government could use average cleanup costs compiled by a state environmental regulator to estimate remediation outlays for leaking underground storage tanks or dry-cleaning fluid spills. However, Statement 49, footnote 14 notes that "the averages should be evaluated to ensure that they are applicable to the polluted site" and that "such averages may not be applicable if the site situation is not common or has unique characteristics."
