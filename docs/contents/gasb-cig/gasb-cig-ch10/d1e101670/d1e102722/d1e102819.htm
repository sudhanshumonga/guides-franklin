Chapter 10, Question 10.13.4

10.13.4.Q—A government plans to issue fixed-rate bonds. Before the issuance date, however, it decides to issue variable-rate bonds and enter into a pay-fixed, receive-variable interest rate swap. Should it still apply hedge accounting if the swap is a hedging derivative instrument even though the government did not originally intend to establish a hedging relationship?
A—Yes. A potential hedging derivative instrument (in this case, the pay-fixed, receive-variable interest rate swap) that meets the hedge effectiveness criteria in paragraphs 26–48 should be reported using the hedge accounting guidance (paragraph 20 of Statement 53, as amended).
