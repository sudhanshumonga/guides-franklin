Chapter 7, Question 7.47.15

7.47.15.Q—What is the difference between "overhead expenses" discussed in Statement 34, paragraph 59 (which should be eliminated), and "administrative overhead charges" referred to in paragraph 43 of that Statement (which need not be eliminated)?
A—There really is no difference in the two terms, but there is a difference in the objectives of the two paragraphs. The intent in paragraph 59 is to eliminate the “doubling up” of internal allocations (that is, reducing the expenses of the allocating function by the amount allocated [the internal “revenue”] to others) so that only one function reports the expense. The concept is similar to accounting for interfund reimbursements. The provision in paragraph 43 is a practical consideration that relieves governments of the burden of removing and reclassifying any “indirect” elements of internal charges from the direct expenses of a function. (See the examples in Questions 7.47.11 and 7.47.14.)
