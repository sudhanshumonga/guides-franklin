Chapter 1, Question 1.14.2

1.14.2.Q—Can an investment adviser or manager be a counterparty for the purpose of determining custodial credit risk?
A—An investment adviser may or may not be a counterparty. If an adviser's only function in the acquisition of investments is to advise on which types of investments or which particular investments to acquire, the adviser is not a counterparty for purposes of custodial credit risk. On the other hand, if the adviser acquires those investment securities for the government, whether working as a broker or through a broker, the adviser is a counterparty.
An investment manager is responsible for managing a portfolio, including acquiring investments, and is a counterparty because of the acquisition function.
