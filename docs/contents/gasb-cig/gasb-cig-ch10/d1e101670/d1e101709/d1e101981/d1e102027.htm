Chapter 10, Question 10.5.2

10.5.2.Q—Does a short sale of securities meet the leverage characteristic of a derivative instrument as defined in paragraphs 11 and 12 of Statement 53?
A—No. The characteristic of leverage is not present. The borrowed security is the initial net investment in the contract, which is not smaller than would be required for other types of contracts that would be expected to have a similar response to changes in market factors (paragraph 11). In other words, a short seller receives the entire fair value of the securities, creating a liability. Likewise, the purchaser pays the entire fair value of the purchased securities.
