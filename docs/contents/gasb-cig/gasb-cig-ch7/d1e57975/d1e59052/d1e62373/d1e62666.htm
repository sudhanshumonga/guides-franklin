Chapter 7, Question 7.22.10

7.22.10.Q—Paragraph 58 of Statement 34 states that internal balances should be eliminated from the total primary government column. However, because some governments have blended component units with different fiscal years, the internal accounts may be "out of balance" at the reporting date due to the timing difference. In those situations, is it acceptable to report an internal balance in the total primary government column?
A—Yes. In those instances, the total primary government column would report an internal balance. Paragraph 60 of Statement 14 requires the reason for the imbalance to be disclosed in the notes.
