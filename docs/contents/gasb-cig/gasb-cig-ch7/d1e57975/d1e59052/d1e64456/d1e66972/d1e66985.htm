Chapter 7, Question 7.46.1

7.46.1.Q—Can a government that wants to provide more detail (more programs or more details of general revenues, for example) than fits on one page divide and report the statement of activities on two pages?
A—Yes. Exhibit 3 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-2 presents a two-page approach for the municipal government that is illustrated in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-1, Illustration A. In this case, the government presents its governmental functions or programs in more detail than is displayed in the fund financial statements. The expanded net program cost information is presented on the first page, and general revenues and changes in net position information is displayed on the second page. The state government illustrated in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-1, Exhibit 33, also uses a two-page approach to provide additional details of its general revenues.
