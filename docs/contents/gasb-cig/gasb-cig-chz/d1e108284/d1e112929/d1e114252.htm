Chapter Z, Question Z.54.38

Z.54.38.Q—A government uses its highest level of decision-making authority to require that 20 percent of an existing revenue source can be spent only for smoking cessation programs. The government would be required to take the same action to redeploy those resources to another use. A separate fund is established to account for those resources and their use. The government budgets the entire revenue source in the general fund and annually appropriates a transfer to the separate fund. Can the government report the separate fund as a special revenue fund?
A—The action taken to limit the use of those resources (the initial action establishing the limitation, not the actions taken to appropriate the transfers) qualifies as a commitment of a specific revenue for a specific purpose. However, notwithstanding the budgetary treatment, the separate fund can be reported as a special revenue fund only if the 20 percent portion is recognized as revenue in the separate fund, rather than in the general fund. (See also Questions Z.54.35 and Z.54.36.)
