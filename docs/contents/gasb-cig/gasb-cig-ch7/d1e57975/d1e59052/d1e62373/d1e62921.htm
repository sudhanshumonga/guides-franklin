Chapter 7, Question 7.22.18

7.22.18.Q—A component unit of a state government issues debt that has the characteristics of conduit debt, as discussed in paragraph 2 of Interpretation 2. However, the proceeds are used to finance construction of capital assets for the state (the primary government). Should the issuance be regarded as conduit debt?
A—No. Paragraph 2 stipulates that the beneficiary of conduit debt should not be “part of the issuer’s financial reporting entity.” The state, as the primary government, is “part of” the component unit’s financial reporting entity. As noted in paragraph 14 of the Basis for Conclusions of Interpretation 2, the intent in paragraph 2 is to exclude intra-entity transactions in which the issuer and the beneficiary are within the same financial reporting entity.
