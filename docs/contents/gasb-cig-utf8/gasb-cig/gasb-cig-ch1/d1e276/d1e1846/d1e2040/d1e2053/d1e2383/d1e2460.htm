Chapter 1, Question 1.16.4

1.16.4.Q—Are investment securities exposed to custodial credit risk if they are both acquired through and held in custody by the same trust department?
A—Yes. The investment securities would be exposed to custodial credit risk because they are held by the counterparty. In this situation, there is no segregation between the acquisition and custody functions. As discussed in Question 1.15.2, custody by the counterparty always results in exposure to custodial credit risk regardless of other relationships.
