Chapter 4, Question 4.47.2

4.47.2.Q—What is joint control?
A—Paragraph 69 of Statement 14 defines joint control to mean that no single participant has the ability to unilaterally control the financial or operating policies of the joint venture. For example, if a joint venture with three participants has a six-member governing board, it would be subject to joint control if each of the participants appoints two members and all members have equivalent voting power. However, if one of the participants appoints a voting majority of the governing board, the organization would not be jointly controlled.
