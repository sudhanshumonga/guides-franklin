Chapter 7, Question 7.104.15

7.104.15.Q—Phase 3 governments are not required to retroactively report infrastructure, but they are encouraged to do so. If a phase 3 government has records to support some, but not all, of its infrastructure assets or has estimated the historical cost of some, but not all, of its infrastructure assets, can the government report those "retroactive" infrastructure assets?
A—A phase 3 government can report a network or networks of "retroactive" infrastructure without all infrastructure assets being required to be reported during its transition period. Phase 3 governments that have not retroactively reported all infrastructure networks are considered to be in transition. Paragraph 150 of Statement 34 provides that in transition, "those networks for which information is available may be reported." Additionally, a phase 3 government in transition is required to make the disclosures specified in paragraph 151 of Statement 34. (See also Question 7.85.5.)
