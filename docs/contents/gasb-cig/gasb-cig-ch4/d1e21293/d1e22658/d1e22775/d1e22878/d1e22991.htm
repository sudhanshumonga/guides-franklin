Chapter 4, Question 4.18.5

4.18.5.Q—A potential component unit's articles of incorporation designate certain primary government officials to be potential component unit board members, and these members represent a voting majority of the potential component unit board. Does this constitute appointment of a voting majority?
A—Yes. Footnote 5 of Statement 14 states that appointment of a voting majority "includes situations in which a voting majority of an organization's governing body consists of the primary government's officials serving as required by law (and, thus, technically not appointed by the primary government)."
For example, a housing authority has five board members, three of which are designated by the articles of incorporation to be the mayor, the city manager, and the city’s economic development director. This would constitute appointment by the city of a voting majority of the housing authority’s board.
