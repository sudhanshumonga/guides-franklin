Chapter 8, Question 8.42.6

8.42.6.Q—Paragraph 25d(3) of Statement 45 requires disclosure that actuarial calculations are based on the types of benefits provided under the terms of the current substantive plan. Is an employer required to disclose or otherwise present information about how it formed a judgment as to the content of the substantive plan (for example, what documents or communications were used as a basis for determination)? If not required to do so, may an employer disclose such information?
A—Statement 45 does not require disclosure of information in the notes to the financial statements about how the terms of the substantive plan were determined. However, an employer may choose to make this disclosure.
