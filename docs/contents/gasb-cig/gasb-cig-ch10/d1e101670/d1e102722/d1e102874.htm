Chapter 10, Question 10.13.6

10.13.6.Q—A governmental utility entered into a derivative instrument to hedge market risk associated with its purchase of natural gas, expected to occur in six months. The hedge was established and the expected transaction occurred all within the same fiscal year. How should the effects of the hedging derivative instrument transaction be reported?
A—If the derivative instrument met the requirements of a hedging derivative instrument (paragraphs 26–31 and 49–62 of Statement 53) and thus was considered part of an effective hedging relationship, the payment provided by the hedging derivative instrument should be reported as an adjustment of the actual transaction (paragraph 25b). See nonauthoritative Appendix B10-2, Illustrations 1.4 and 2.4.
On the other hand, if the derivative instrument does not meet the requirements of a hedging derivative instrument, the derivative instrument should be considered an investment derivative instrument. The payment provided by the derivative instrument should be reported as investment revenue (paragraph 20).
