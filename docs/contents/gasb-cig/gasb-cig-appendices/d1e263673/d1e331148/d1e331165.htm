Example 1

CIG Appendix Z-1, Example 1
Assume the following facts:
•A town decides to reduce its parks and recreation department staff by eliminating 15 current positions.
•The plan of termination approved by the town council provides that each terminated employee will receive a single cash payment of $5,000.
•The payment will be made on the date the employee ceases service and will be paid regardless of when the employee leaves service during the period from June 15 to September 15, 20X1.
•On June 15, 20X1, the town notifies employees in the department that in accordance with the plan, terminations will occur on September 15, 20X1.
•The employees whose positions will be terminated are specifically identified on July 1, 20X1.
Following is a summary of the treatment for when the employer should first recognize a liability and expense with regard to these termination benefits:
The scenario described is an example of an involuntary termination benefit with no future service requirement. In this case, the plan of involuntary termination meets the criteria of paragraph 14 of Statement 47. That is, it identifies the number, functions, and location of the employees to be terminated, as well as when the terminations will occur. In addition, the plan is sufficiently detailed to enable employees to determine the type and amount of benefits they will receive if they are involuntarily terminated. Therefore, in accordance with paragraph 13 of Statement 47, the town should recognize a liability and expense related to the involuntary termination benefit when the plan is communicated to employees (June 15, 20X1).
