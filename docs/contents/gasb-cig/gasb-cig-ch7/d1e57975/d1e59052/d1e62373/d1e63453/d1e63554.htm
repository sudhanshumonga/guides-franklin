Chapter 7, Question 7.24.4

7.24.4.Q—How precise should the specific purpose be for resources to be considered restricted in the statement of net position?
A—The determination of whether resources should be classified as restricted in the government-wide statement of net position begins at the fund level. The purpose can be broad or narrow and still be considered restricted, as long as the constraints meet the definition in paragraph 34 of Statement 34, as amended. However, the purpose should not be so broad that, in substance, it establishes no purpose restrictions narrower than the general restriction of the activity. For instance, resources that are required to be used for "governmental activities" are not considered restricted in the general fund. Similarly, resources that are required by a rate payer agreement to be used only for “water and sewer fund purposes” should not be classified as restricted in the water and sewer fund. (See Question 7.24.28 about reporting restricted net position for business-type activities.)
