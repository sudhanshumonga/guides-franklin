Chapter 10—Appendix 2, Illustration 1.1

On July 1, 20X0, a city issues variable-rate demand bonds of $100 million. The bonds mature on June 18, 20X4. The coupon is remarketed weekly. For the purposes of this illustration, that remarketed rate is considered to be the SIFMA swap index plus the state tax difference—10 basis points.
At the same time, the city enters into a $100 million notional, pay-fixed, receive-variable interest rate swap. The variable payment rate on the swap also resets weekly. The variable rate is the SIFMA swap index. The fixed rate is 3.807 percent, and the swap terminates on June 11, 20X4. Upon association with the variable-rate bonds (at the inception of the swap), the fair value of the swap is zero.
