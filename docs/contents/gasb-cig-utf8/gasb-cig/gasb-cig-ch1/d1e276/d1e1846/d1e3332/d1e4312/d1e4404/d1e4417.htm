Chapter 1, Question 1.40.1

1.40.1.Q—What are the "money market" accounts offered by financial institutions? Are they like money market mutual funds, which are not exposed to custodial credit risk?
A—Financial institution "money market" accounts are simply deposits that pay interest at a rate that is set to make the accounts competitive with money market mutual funds. These money market accounts should be treated like any other deposit account for purposes of custodial credit risk.
