Landing

Maximum airspeed for lowering the gear is 312 KEAS, based on landing gear structural limits.
Maximum speed at landing is based on the ground speed certified tire speed limit. The maximum predicted ground speed at touchdown should not exceed 214 knots.
Approach and landing begin at about 10,000 feet from the touchdown site. The approach speed is based on the glideslope. Approach and landing guidance is provided from 7,500 feet and braking is modulated to hold the reference velocity.
