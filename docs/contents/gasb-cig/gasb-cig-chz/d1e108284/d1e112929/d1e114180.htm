Chapter Z, Question Z.54.35

Z.54.35.Q—A state government maintains a Lottery Operations enterprise fund to account for the operations of the state lottery, including the payment of prizes and the distribution of the net lottery proceeds to other state funds for spending in accordance with constitutional requirements. The Lottery Operations fund recognizes lottery sales revenue and reports the distributions to other funds as transfers. In the receiving funds, do the transfers in constitute a restricted revenue source so that they can be reported as special revenue funds?
A—No. The restricted revenues are recognized in the Lottery Operations fund; therefore, the amounts transferred to other funds do not constitute "revenue" in the other funds.
