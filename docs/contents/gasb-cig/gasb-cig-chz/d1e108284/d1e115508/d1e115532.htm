Chapter Z, Question Z.I1.1

Z.I1.1.Q—What are examples of bonds that may be demand bonds?
A—A demand bond provides the bondholder with the right to demand that the government-issuer redeem the bond within a short period—for example, within 12 months of the date of the statement of net position. The demand provision may be described as a tender option or a put option. A demand provision may be included in tender options bonds, puttable bonds, extendible maturity commercial paper, extendible maturity bonds, variable rate demand obligations, or securities with proprietary labels.
