Chapter Z, Question Z.42.10

Z.42.10.Q—A new regulation regarding coal-fired generating facilities is approved that requires either that only low-sulfur coal be burned or that certain scrubbers be installed. The regulation does not prohibit continued operation of the facilities; however, the additional costs of low-sulfur coal or of the installation of scrubbers may make it no longer economically feasible to continue to operate a facility. Is adoption of this regulation an indicator of impairment for coal-fired generating facilities?
A—No. Adoption of the regulation is not an indicator of impairment for coal-fired generating facilities, because the regulation does not directly reduce the service utility of these facilities. However, if management decides to close a facility to avoid the costs of complying with the regulation, the decision to close the facility would be an indicator of impairment and would precipitate an impairment evaluation.
