Chapter 8, Question 8.6.1

8.6.1.Q—An employer is required to contribute to a defined benefit pension plan. State law directs that "excess earnings" (as defined in the state law) are allocated to provide retiree healthcare benefits. The level of healthcare benefits is determined by the plan trustees. For financial accounting and reporting purposes, does the employer have any costs or obligations to report associated with the retiree healthcare benefit (assuming that "excess earnings" have occurred and healthcare benefits have been provided at some point)?
A—Yes. When an employer contributes to a defined benefit pension plan that administers a retiree healthcare benefit, GASB standards require that the employer separately account for its costs and obligations related to two plans—a pension plan (in accordance with Statement 68, as amended, or Statement 73, as applicable) and an OPEB plan (in accordance with Statement 45, as amended).
