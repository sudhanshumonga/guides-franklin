Chapter 10, Question 10.13.3

10.13.3.Q—A government issues variable-rate bonds, and a pay-fixed, receive-variable interest rate swap is entered into by the government. The swap is an effective hedging derivative instrument. Documentation of the government’s intention contemporaneous with these transactions, however, was not prepared. Should hedge accounting be applied?
A—Yes. Documentation of the hedging relationship is not a criterion for the application of hedge accounting. If the hedging relationship meets the criteria found in paragraphs 26–48 of Statement 53, hedge accounting should be applied.
