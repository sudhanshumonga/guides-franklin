Chapter 8, Question 8.41.1

8.41.1.Q—For employer accounting and reporting purposes, is an employee and retiree healthcare plan a cost-sharing plan with regard to retiree healthcare benefits if the participating employers' groups are experience rated as a single pool and blended premium rates are applied to both active employees and retirees?
A—Not necessarily. The characteristics mentioned do not determine whether a healthcare plan is a cost-sharing plan for employer OPEB accounting purposes. First, the plan is required to be administered as a qualifying trust, or equivalent arrangement, as discussed in paragraph 13g(3) of Statement 45. In addition, the classification of the plan as cost-sharing for accounting purposes relates not to the current-period insurance premium structure used but to whether:
•The employers in a plan have committed to share their annual OPEB costs measured as required by Statement 45, total actuarial accrued liabilities related to past services, assets accumulated in the plan, and unfunded actuarial accrued liabilities—in which case the plan should be classified as a cost-sharing plan, or
• The employers remain individually responsible for funding their own unfunded obligations—in which case the plan should be classified as an agent multiple-employer plan.
