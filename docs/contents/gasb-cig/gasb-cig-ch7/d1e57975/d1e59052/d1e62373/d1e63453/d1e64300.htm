Chapter 7, Question 7.24.29

7.24.29.Q—A local benefactor has made a $5 million donation to the community college. According to the terms of the bequest, the principal of the gift is required to remain intact and the earnings are required to be used for improvements to the college’s fine arts center. At the end of the current fiscal year, the fair value of the endowment has dropped to $4.85 million. Because the original amount is nonexpendable, should the college report restricted nonexpendable net position of $5 million or $4.85 million? If the market recovers in the following year and the endowment value increases to $5.15 million, what amounts are reported in the restricted nonexpendable component of net position and restricted expendable component of net position?
A—In the current year, restricted nonexpendable net position should be reported at $4.85 million. Restricted net position should represent the reported amount of restricted assets reduced by liabilities related to those assets. (See Questions 7.24.7 and 7.24.13 and nonauthoritative Exercise 3 in nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3.) In this example, the restricted assets reported in the statement of net position are $4.85 million as a result of the investment loss.
If the market recovers in the following year and the endowment value increases to $5.15 million, the community college would restore the restricted nonexpendable component of net position to $5 million and increase the restricted expendable component of net position by $150,000.
