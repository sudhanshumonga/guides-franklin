Chapter 10, Question 10.13.9

10.13.9Q—An interest rate swap is an effective hedging derivative instrument of a government’s debt. On the statement of net position, should the swap be reported as an element of that debt, similar to unamortized debt premium or discount?
A—No. Unlike an unamortized debt premium or discount which “follows the debt,” an effective hedging derivative instrument is a result of a separate transaction entered into by a government. The manner in which those transactions should be reported depends upon whether the hedging derivative instrument is an asset or liability (paragraph 19 of Statement 53, as amended).
