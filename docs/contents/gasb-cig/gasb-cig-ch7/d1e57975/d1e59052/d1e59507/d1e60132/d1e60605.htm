Chapter 7, Question 7.12.17

7.12.17.Q—A city purchased land from another government for the sum of $1. How should the city report this acquisition?
A—The purchase price of $1 does not meet the definition of an exchange transaction as defined in paragraph 1 of Statement 33. An exchange transaction is one in which each party receives and gives up essentially equal values. The substance of the transaction is that the city received a donation of land (a nonexchange transaction), and the city should report the land at acquisition value as required by paragraph 79 of Statement 72.
