Chapter 7, Question 7.24.30

7.24.30.Q—How should an institution report a permanent endowment that was established with an original gift of $1 million, with the earnings restricted for a specific purpose, that has a fair value of $1.2 million at the end of the fiscal year? If the market subsequently declines in fair value to $950,000, what amount should be reported in the restricted nonexpendable component of net position and how is the $250,000 market decline recorded?
A—The $1 million will be reported as a restricted nonexpendable component of net position and $200,000 as restricted expendable net position. If the $1.2 million gift subsequently declines in fair value to $950,000, the $250,000 decline in value is recorded as a $200,000 reduction in the restricted expendable component of net position and a $50,000 reduction in the restricted nonexpendable component of net position (consistent with the guidance in Question 7.24.29) for a final value of $950,000 in the restricted nonexpendable component of net position.
