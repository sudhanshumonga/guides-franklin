Chapter 9, Question 9.29.3

9.29.3.Q—If a government cannot obtain information about the revenue base from which the governmental activities debt of an overlapping government is repaid, how should the percentage of overlap be computed?
A—Paragraph 28 of Statement 44 offers population and personal income as possible substitutes for determining the percentage of overlap between two governments. Property values and retail sales are other possible bases that a government might use.
