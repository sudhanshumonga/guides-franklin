Chapter 7, Question 7.97.3

7.97.3.Q—A primary government is a special-purpose government engaged only in business-type activities but has component units (that do not meet the requirements for blending) that are engaged in governmental activities. How should the component units' information be presented?
A—Even though the primary government is a special-purpose government engaged only in business-type activities, the financial reporting entity is not. Therefore, the entity should present government-wide statements as provided for in paragraph 135 of Statement 34.
