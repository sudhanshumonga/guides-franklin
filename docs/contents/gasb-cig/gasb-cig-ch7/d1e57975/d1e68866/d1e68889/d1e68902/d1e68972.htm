Chapter 7, Question 7.55.3

7.55.3.Q—Is it allowable to report a particular fund (a capital projects fund, for example) as a major fund for only one or two years?
A—Yes. Although many funds will continue to pass the major fund test year after year, capital projects funds often experience uneven expenditure levels and may exceed the major fund percentages for only a brief period (or sporadically over a longer period). Governments can, however, in the interest of consistency, choose to report a fund as a major fund even if it does not meet the percentage criteria.
