Chapter Z, Question Z.54.3

Z.54.3.Q—Should the portion of fund balance that is attributable to unrealized gains be included in nonspendable fund balance?
A—No. The unrealized gain attributable to a specific investment should be included in the same fund balance classification as the investment principal. The ability to "spend" unrealized gains generally is the same as the ability to spend the principal of the fair-valued investment. Paragraph 57 in the Basis for Conclusions of Statement 31, states that governmental fund investments-and changes in the fair value of those investments-are appropriately available and thus merit recognition in governmental fund financial statements. Consequently, the unrealized gain attributable to a specific investment should be included in the same fund balance classification as the investment principal. (See Question Z.54.49 about spendable appreciation of principal of permanent fund investments.)
