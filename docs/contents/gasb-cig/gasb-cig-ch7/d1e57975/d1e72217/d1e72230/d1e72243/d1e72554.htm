Chapter 7, Question 7.91.11

7.91.11.Q—Budgeted revenue forecasts are provided by a state's legislative budget office for use in determining expenditure levels for all legislatively appropriated funds. Those estimated revenues, however, are not contained in the appropriations bill and thus are not "legally adopted." Should the state report those revenue estimates as budgeted revenues in the budget-to-actual comparison?
A—Yes. The estimated revenues should be included in the budgetary comparison as budgeted revenues. Paragraph 130 of Statement 34 requires that actual results be compared with amounts in the "appropriated budget." Paragraph 11 in NCGA Interpretation 10, as amended, which is referenced in footnote 55 of Statement 34, states that the appropriated budget is defined as "the expenditure authority created by the appropriation bills or ordinances which are signed into law and related estimated revenues." The reference to estimated revenues after the mention of the legal adoption of the appropriations bill implies that the revenue estimates need not be "legally adopted."
