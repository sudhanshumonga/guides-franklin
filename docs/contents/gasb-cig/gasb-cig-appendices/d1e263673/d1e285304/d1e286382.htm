Case 28—Music Instruction Program
Facts: A school district did not have a music program as part of its curriculum. Several parents formed a group and hired a music instructor who was not an employee of the school district. In addition, the parents' group entered into an agreement with the district to rent classroom space after school hours to hold the music lessons. The parents share the expenses of the program based on a per-child allocation of the total cost. The rental payments made to the school district were insignificant to the district.
Conclusion: The music program is not a component unit of the school district because the parents' group that established the after-school music program did not separately incorporate or apply for tax exemption. Thus, there is no "organization" to which the three criteria for inclusion would be applied.
