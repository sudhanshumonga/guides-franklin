Chapter 7, Question 7.22.16

7.22.16.Q—How should a government report the correction of an error in previously issued financial statements?
A—A correction of an error is addressed in Statement 62, paragraph 88 and paragraph 89, as amended, which requires that the correction of an error be reported as restatements of beginning net position/fund balances, not as a separately identified cumulative effect in the current-period statement of activities or proprietary fund statement of revenues, expenses, and changes in fund net position. Paragraph 88 of Statement 62 states that a correction of an error should be reported as a prior period adjustment. Consistent with footnote 13 of Statement 34, as amended, governments should implement that requirement by either displaying the amount on the face of the statement as an adjustment to beginning net position/fund balance, as previously reported, or by displaying beginning net position/fund balances, as restated, with an explanation of the restatement in the notes to the financial statements. When comparative statements are presented, corresponding adjustments should be made to the transactions and balances for all periods presented to incorporate the retroactive application of the prior period adjustments, as discussed in paragraph 61 of Statement 62, as amended.
