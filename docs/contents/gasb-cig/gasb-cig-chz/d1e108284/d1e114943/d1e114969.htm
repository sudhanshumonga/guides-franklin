Chapter Z, Question Z.60.1

Z.60.1.Q—How specific should a service concession agreement be to satisfy the criteria in paragraph 4c of Statement 60, that require the transferor to determine or have the ability to modify or approve what services the operator is required to provide, and to whom the operator is required to provide the services?
A—Often the activity that is subject to a service concession arrangement, by its nature, addresses those criteria and specific reference in the agreement is not necessary. For example, a public golf course provides golfing facilities/services to the general public or citizens of the community and their guests—answering the questions of what services are provided and to whom. Similarly, a toll road, by its nature, provides a medium for public vehicular traffic to operators of those vehicles.
