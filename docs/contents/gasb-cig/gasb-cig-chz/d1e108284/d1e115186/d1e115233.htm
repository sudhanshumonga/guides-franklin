Chapter Z, Question Z.69.2

Z.69.2.Q—A village and township enter into an agreement through which the township government will cease to exist and will become part of the village’s legal entity. Some of the township’s capital assets will not be used by the village after the merger and should be evaluated for impairment, as required by paragraph 26 of Statement 69. How should the impairment adjustment be presented?
A—Paragraph 56 of Statement 69 requires disclosure of explanations of adjustments, including those related to capital asset impairments. The carrying value of the capital assets, as reported by the township prior to the merger, the amount of the impairment adjustment, and an explanation of the adjustment should be disclosed so that readers of the village’s financial statements understand the difference between the carrying values of the township’s assets before the merger and the initial amounts reported by the village after the merger.
