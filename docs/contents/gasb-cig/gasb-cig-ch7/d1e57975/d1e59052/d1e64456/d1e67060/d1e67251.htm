Chapter 7, Question 7.47.7

7.47.7.Q—The assets and liabilities of a city’s internal service fund are reported with governmental activities in the government-wide statement of net position. However, the “look-back” adjustment for the internal service fund includes a significant “undercharge” involving the water utility enterprise fund. As a result, the internal balances reported in the statement of net position are adjusted, as illustrated in Exercise 4 of nonauthoritative Appendix B7-3. How does this year’s internal balance adjustment affect the statement of net position next year?
A—That portion of a look-back adjustment that crosses activity classes is referred to as the “crossover” amount because some of the change statement expenses are allocated to one activity class (business-type), but all of the net position is reported in the other activity class (governmental). The accumulated cross-over adjustments should be carried forward from the prior year’s statement of net position. The accumulated internal balance arising from prior years’ crossover look-back adjustments is recognized in the reconciliations of government-wide net position to fund balances/net position in the funds. The adjustment is similar to the reconciling items needed to report governmental capital assets and liabilities—that is, to report balances that are included in the government-wide statements, but not in the funds. The reconciliations for both governmental and enterprise funds will need to include this item, because interfund balances (in the fund financial statements) are not adjusted for the look-back amounts. (See also Question 7.47.24.)
