Chapter 10, Question 10.44.3

10.44.3.Q—A government enters into a hybrid instrument represented by an up-front payment of $2 million and an at-the-market swap with a fixed rate of 3 percent. The hybrid instrument requires the government to make fixed-rate payments of 5 percent. The government intends to employ the at-the-market swap as a hedging derivative instrument. For purposes of evaluating hedge effectiveness, what terms should be used?
A—The terms of the at-the-market swap should be employed to evaluate effectiveness. For example, if the hedge is evaluated using the synthetic instrument method, the rate of 3 percent should be used as the fixed rate of the at-the-market swap.
