Chapter 7, Question 7.50.7

7.50.7.Q—Are states that report unemployment compensation funds required to follow all of the provisions of Statement 10, as amended, including the requirement to make risk-management-type note disclosures for the changes in claims liabilities?
A—No. Footnote 34 to paragraph 67b of Statement 34 requires that state unemployment compensation funds be reported in enterprise funds. While public entity risk-pools also are required to be reported in enterprise funds, Statement 34, as amended, does not require that state unemployment compensation funds follow the provisions for public entity risk pools in Statement 10, as amended. Paragraph 391 in the Basis for Conclusions of Statement 34 explains the reasons for requiring governments to use the enterprise fund model for unemployment compensation funds and refers to risk-retention operations as “similar.” However, it is not meant to change the way governments (that are already using enterprise funds) report their unemployment compensation programs.
