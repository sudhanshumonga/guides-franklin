Chapter 7, Question 7.22.3

7.22.3.Q—If the "order of liquidity" approach is used in the statement of net position, where should restricted assets be reported?
A—Paragraph 31 of Statement 34 indicates that an asset's liquidity is affected by restrictions that may limit the government's ability to use it. If the restrictions are short-lived—for example, cash that is held in a bond and interest reserve account that is required to be used to pay current maturities—the cash could be reported with unrestricted cash. On the other hand, if the conditions underlying the restrictions are such that they will not be met in the short or medium term, restricted assets would be reported lower (as less liquid) in the statement of net position. Permanently restricted resources, for example, are essentially as illiquid as capital assets. Term restrictions may indicate that the assets should be reported at approximately the same liquidity level as long-term receivables, depending on the length of the restrictions. (See Question 7.71.1 about how to report restricted assets using a "classified" approach in a proprietary fund statement of net position. See also Question 7.22.2 about how to report restricted assets of governmental activities in the government-wide statement of net position using the classified format.)
