Chapter 2, Question 2.29.3

2.29.3.Q—If an internal service fund purchases supplies from both vendors and other funds of the primary government, should it report its purchases from other funds separately from its purchases from vendors?
A—Paragraph 31 of Statement 9, as amended, states that interfund services provided and used should be presented separately from other operating transactions. The intent was to highlight interfund activity. Although the cash flows between the internal service fund and other funds of the government may seem identical to transactions with outside suppliers or vendors, the fact that the activity is "intra-entity" is noteworthy.
