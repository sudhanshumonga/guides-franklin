Chapter 4, Question 4.55.1

4.55.1.Q—Is it possible for an organization with characteristics of a joint venture to be a component unit in a reporting entity's financial statements?
A—Yes. The organization may be fiscally dependent on one of the participating governments, and have a financial benefit or burden relationship with that participant, thus making it a component unit of that participant. For example, the joint venture agreement may stipulate that debt cannot be issued by the joint venture without the express approval of a specific participating government. (See Sections 4.9–4.12 for further discussion of fiscal dependence.)
