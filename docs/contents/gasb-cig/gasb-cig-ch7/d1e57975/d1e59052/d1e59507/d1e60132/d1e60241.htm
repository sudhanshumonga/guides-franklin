Chapter 7, Question 7.12.5

7.12.5.Q—A city requires developers of new residential neighborhoods to build the infrastructure and then donate it to the city at the completion of the project. How should the city determine the cost of those assets?
A—Paragraph 79 of Statement 72 requires that donated capital assets be reported at acquisition value, which is determined as of the date of donation. In the example addressed in this question, the developer’s cost for the infrastructure in new residential neighborhoods would be an acceptable method that could be used to determine acquisition value, but other methods—for example, an estimate of the cost associated with the construction of the infrastructure made by the city’s public works department—also may be acceptable.
