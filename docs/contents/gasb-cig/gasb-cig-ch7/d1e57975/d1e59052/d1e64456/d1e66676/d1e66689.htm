Chapter 7, Question 7.42.1

7.42.1.Q—A local government receives a large bequest from the estate of a wealthy benefactor. The corpus of the donation cannot be spent but instead is required to be invested to provide earnings that are restricted to a special use. Because the principal amount can never be spent, how should it be reported?
A—The government should account for the bequest in a permanent fund. (See Question 7.49.3.) In the governmental fund statements, the donation should be reported as revenue and ultimately would be included in nonspendable fund balance. For the government-wide statement of activities, paragraph 53 of Statement 34, as amended, requires contributions to permanent funds to be reported separately from, but in the same manner as, general revenues. That is, these sources of financing the net cost of the government’s programs should be reported at the bottom of the statement of activities to arrive at the all-inclusive change in net position for the period. In the government-wide statement of net position, the corpus of the donation should be included in the restricted component of net position, nonexpendable. (See also Question 7.24.6 about how permanent endowments are reported in net position.)
