Chapter 10, Question 10.16.2

10.16.2.Q—A government issues bonds in the amount of $100 million. Simultaneously, it enters into an interest rate swap that has a notional amount of $101 million. May the swap be employed as a hedging derivative instrument when the notional amount of the swap is greater than the principal amount of the hedgeable item?
A—Yes, provided that the hedging relationship passes one of the effectiveness methods in paragraphs 31–48 of Statement 53. The entire notional amount of the derivative instrument and the principal amount should be considered when evaluating hedge effectiveness.
