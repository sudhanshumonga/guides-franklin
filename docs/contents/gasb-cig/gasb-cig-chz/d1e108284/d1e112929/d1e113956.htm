Chapter Z, Question Z.54.26

Z.54.26.Q—By exercising its highest level of decision-making authority, a government has established a Budgetary Stabilization Fund and imposed a requirement that 15 percent of certain mineral rights royalties received should be set aside to provide for budgetary imbalances. That decision can only be reversed or modified by the government taking the same action. The conditions under which the resources can be used are sufficiently prescriptive to be reported as committed fund balance. Can the Budgetary Stabilization Fund be reported as a special revenue fund?
A—Yes. Because the foundation of the separate fund is a specific committed revenue source , it can be reported as a special revenue fund, provided that the revenues are recognized in the separate fund.
