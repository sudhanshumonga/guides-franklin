Chapter 1, Question 1.39.3

1.39.3.Q—What disclosure requirements apply to a negotiable certificate of deposit?
A—A negotiable certificate of deposit is a form of a participating interest-earning investment contract whose value generally is affected by market (interest rate) changes (paragraph 8 of Statement 31). For a negotiable certificate of deposit that is measured at fair value, the investment risk disclosures of Statement 40, as amended, and the fair value disclosures of Statement 72 should be made. For a negotiable certificate of deposit that is measured at amortized cost, the investment risk disclosures of Statement 40, as amended, should be made.
