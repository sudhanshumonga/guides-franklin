Example 2

CIG Appendix Z-1, Example 2
Assume the following facts:
•A city decides to privatize its sanitation operations and therefore determines that it will no longer need to employ the 25 employees that currently staff that department.
•The city council approves a plan to terminate the positions as of September 30, 20X1.
•Because the city needs to retain employees in order to maintain sanitation operations until September 30, 20X1, the plan of termination provides that each employee that renders service until September 30, 20X1 will receive a termination benefit in the form of a one-time cash payment of $7,500.
•An employee that leaves voluntarily before September 30, 20X1 will not be eligible to receive any portion of the benefit.
•The plan is communicated to the employees on March 31, 20X1.
•The city estimates that 20 of the sanitation department employees will remain employed to collect the termination benefit payment.
Following is a summary of what the employer should recognize as a liability and expense with regard to these termination benefits in financial statements for the year ended June 30, 20X1:
This is an involuntary termination plan that meets the criteria of paragraph 14 of Statement 47. That is, it identifies the number, functions, and location of the employees to be terminated, as well as when the terminations will occur. In addition, the plan is sufficiently detailed to enable employees to determine the type and amount of benefits they will receive if they are involuntarily terminated. Because the plan has a future service requirement, the city is required by paragraph 15 of Statement 47 to recognize a liability and expense for the involuntary termination benefits ratably over the period from the point in time when the plan was approved and communicated and the amounts could be estimated (March 31, 20X1) to the end of the required future service period (September 30, 20X1). Therefore, in financial statements for the year ended June 30, 20X1, the employer should recognize a pro rata portion of the liability and expense for the costs related to the 20 employees. Because the termination plan is directly associated with a disposal of government operations, only termination costs recognized subsequent to June 30, 20X1, would be included in the gain or loss from the disposal of operations as of September 30, 20X1, in accordance with footnote 7 to paragraph 53a of Statement No. 69, Government Combinations and Disposals of Government Operations.
