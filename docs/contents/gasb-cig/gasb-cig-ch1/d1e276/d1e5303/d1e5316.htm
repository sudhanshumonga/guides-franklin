Chapter 1, Question 1.57.1

1.57.1.Q—Statement 40 specifies five interest rate risk disclosure methods. May a method be used that is not one of the five?
A—No. The GASB's research indicates that the five interest rate risk disclosure methods prescribed in paragraph 15 of Statement 40 are commonly used in practice. The selection among the five methods was permitted to allow a government to choose a disclosure method that is most consistent with the manner in which the government manages its interest rate risk. Because these five methods can adequately communicate a government's interest rate risk exposure, no additional alternatives are permitted.
