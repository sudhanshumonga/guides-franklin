Chapter 5, Question 5.122.5

5.122.5.Q—If an employer’s contributions to a pension plan are reimbursed through a federal grant, should this be accounted for as a special funding situation with the grantor as a nonemployer contributing entity?
A—No. Among other conditions, paragraph 15 of Statement 68 specifies that in order to be a special funding situation, the nonemployer contributing entity is required to make contributions directly to the pension plan. The federal grant is provided to the employer as a reimbursement of the employer’s direct contributions to the pension plan. Therefore, the circumstances do not meet the definition of a special funding situation.
