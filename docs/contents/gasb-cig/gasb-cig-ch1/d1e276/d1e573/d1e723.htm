Chapter 1, Question 1.3.6

1.3.6.Q—Sometimes it may not be readily apparent what a government's different investment types are by the labels that are presented in the note disclosure. If this is the case, should the government provide some explanation about those investment types?
A—If a term is not well understood in common usage or if users in general would not understand the nature of a particular investment type by the label it has been given, preparers should provide a brief description or use a different term that would be more widely understood. For example, an investment fund that purchases companies through leveraged buyouts and is presented in the note disclosures as "leveraged buyouts" or "LBO" could be described as a "leveraged buyout fund."
