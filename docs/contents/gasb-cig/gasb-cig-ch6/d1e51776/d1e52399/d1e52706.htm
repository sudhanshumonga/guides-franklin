Chapter 5, Question 6.11.14

6.11.14Q—Can different governments arrive at different conclusions regarding the classification of similar assets?
A—Yes. A government’s purpose for acquiring a particular asset is key to identifying it as an investment. For example, a retirement plan may own a building whose present service capacity is based solely on its ability to generate cash. In that circumstance, the building is classified as an investment. On the other hand, a local government may own a building that is rented to individuals in the provision of low-income housing. In that circumstance, the building is not considered an investment.
