Chapter 7, Question 7.24.27

7.24.27.Q—A state university's donor agreement addresses the disposition of earnings on permanent endowments. The agreement allows the university to either spend the earnings or reinvest a portion, and if reinvested, the reinvested amount becomes part of the permanent endowment, subject to the same restrictions as the original corpus. How should net position associated with the reinvested earnings be classified in the university's statement of net position?
A—The net position associated with the reinvested earnings should be included in the calculation of nonexpendable restricted net position. The donor has given the university's administration the power to reinvest a portion of the earnings and after that event has occurred, the dollars so reinvested become restricted and the decision to reinvest cannot be reversed.
