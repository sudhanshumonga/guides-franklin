Chapter 9, Question 9.38.1

9.38.1.Q—Should the operating information required by paragraph 39 of Statement 44, as amended, be presented in the statistical sections of financial reports issued separately by pension and OPEB plans in addition to or in lieu of the information required by paragraphs 36–38 of that Statement?
A—Paragraph 104 of the Basis for Conclusions of Statement 44 explains that information about government employees, operating indicators, and capital assets was not considered to be particularly relevant to pension and OPEB plans. The Board requires instead that pension and OPEB plans present information about retired members by type of benefit, average benefit payments, and principal participating employers (if applicable). However, if a pension or OPEB plan believes that presenting any of the information described in paragraphs 36–38 would be meaningful to the user of its financial report, it is allowed (but not required) to do so as long it helps to achieve the objectives of operating information described in paragraph 6 of Statement 44.
