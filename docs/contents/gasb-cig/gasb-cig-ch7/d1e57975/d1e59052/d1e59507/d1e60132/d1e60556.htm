Chapter 7, Question 7.12.15

7.12.15.Q—If both the state and a local government pay jointly toward the construction of a bridge and ownership of the bridge is unclear, what should be capitalized by the locality?
A—When ownership is unclear, the government with primary responsibility for managing the asset should report it. If the local government was solely responsible for maintaining the bridge at the conclusion of its construction, for example, the local government would report the entire historical cost of the bridge (that is, the costs incurred by both the state and the local government).
