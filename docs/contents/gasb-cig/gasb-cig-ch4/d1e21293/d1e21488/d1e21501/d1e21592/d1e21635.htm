Chapter 4, Question 4.7.2

4.7.2.Q—Officials are simultaneously elected to serve on the governing boards of two legally separate organizations. For example, county commissioners are elected to serve on the county board of commissioners and, as a result of that election, also serve as the county parks and recreation board. Would both governing boards be considered separately elected?
A—No. There would be no difference between a board that is serving in a dual capacity based on a single election and a board consisting of primary government officials serving "ex officio," or as required by law. In this example, only the county board of commissioners would be considered separately elected.
Note: References to "primary government," other than in the preceding questions pertaining to definitions, are equally applicable to other stand-alone governments and component units in their separately issued financial statements.
