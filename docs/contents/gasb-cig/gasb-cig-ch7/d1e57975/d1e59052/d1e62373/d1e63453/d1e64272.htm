Chapter 7, Question 7.24.28

7.24.28.Q—Should unrestricted net position reported in an enterprise fund be reclassified as restricted net position of business-type activities in the government-wide statements because the scope of business-type activities is broader than the single purpose of an individual enterprise fund? For example, should unrestricted net position of a water utility fund be considered “restricted to water utility operations” when that fund is combined with other funds to comprise business-type activities in the government-wide statements?
A—No. Even though a statute, ordinance, or rate-payer agreement may limit the use of water fund revenues to water fund purposes, net position in the government-wide statements should be classified as they are in the enterprise fund financial statements. Because the business-type activities reporting unit in government-wide statements does not have a “purpose,” per se, the issue of whether resources in an individual enterprise fund can only be used for a narrower purpose should not be considered. (See also Question 7.24.4.)
