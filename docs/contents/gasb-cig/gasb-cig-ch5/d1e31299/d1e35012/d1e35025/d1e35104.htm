Chapter 5, Question 5.116.3

5.116.3.Q—An employer provides pensions to its employees through a cost-sharing defined benefit pension plan that (a) is administered through a trust that has the characteristics identified in paragraph 4 of Statement 68 and (b) does not have the characteristics in paragraph 2 of Statement No. 78, Pensions Provided through Certain Multiple-Employer Defined Benefit Pension Plans. There is no requirement that the employer make annual contributions to the plan because the employer has a special funding situation (as defined by paragraph 15 of Statement 68) in which the nonemployer contributing entity is the only entity with a legal requirement to make contributions. The employer has no payables to the pension plan, and contributions to the plan are not made by any other nonemployer entities. Does Statement 68, as amended, apply to the employer?
A—Yes. Because the employer has a special funding situation for benefits provided through a pension plan that (a) is administered through a trust that has the characteristics identified in paragraph 4 of Statement 68 and (b) does not have the characteristics in paragraph 2 of Statement 78, Statement 68, as amended, applies. In this circumstance, the cost-sharing employer should apply the requirements of paragraphs 18, 19, and 94–96 of Statement 68, as amended, to recognize pension expense/expenditure and revenue for the support of the nonemployer contributing entity, as well as the requirements for note disclosures and RSI in paragraphs 74–82 of Statement 68, as amended.
