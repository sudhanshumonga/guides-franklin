Chapter 5, Question 5.116.4

5.116.4.Q—In the past, an employer provided pensions to its employees through a cost-sharing defined benefit pension plan that (a) is administered through a trust that has the characteristics identified in paragraph 4 of Statement 68 and (b) does not have the characteristics in paragraph 2 of Statement 78. The employer no longer provides benefits to active employees through the plan. Does Statement 68, as amended, apply?
A—If the cost-sharing employer has no requirement to make contributions to the plan, does not have a payable to the pension plan, and does not receive support from a nonemployer contributing entity through contributions made directly to the pension plan (whether as a result of a special funding situation or not), the requirements of Statement 68, as amended, do not apply to the financial reporting by the employer.
