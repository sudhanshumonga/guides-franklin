Chapter 5, Question 5.62.2

5.62.2.Q—If, instead of crediting interest to the plan members’ accounts at a specified rate of return, the benefit terms described in Question 5.62.1 provide that interest on plan members’ account balances is determined based on an outside index, how should the pension be classified for accounting and financial reporting purposes?
A—Unless the investments of each plan member’s account mirror the investments that comprise the outside index, the crediting of interest earnings based on a rate that is tied to the performance of an outside index does not represent actual earnings on investments in the plan members’ accounts, and the pension should be classified as defined benefit for purposes of applying Statement 67, as amended.
