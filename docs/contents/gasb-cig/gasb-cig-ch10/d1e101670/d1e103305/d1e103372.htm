Chapter 10, Question 10.16.3

10.16.3.Q—A government has variable-rate, tax-exempt debt outstanding. At a later date, the government enters into a basis swap. Under the terms of the swap, the government makes payments based on the SIFMA swap index and receives payments based on the one-month LIBOR index. Is the basis swap a potential hedging derivative instrument?
A—No, this is an investment derivative instrument. There is no identified financial risk being hedged by the basis swap that can be expressed in terms of exposure to adverse changes in cash flows or fair values (paragraph 26 of Statement 53).
