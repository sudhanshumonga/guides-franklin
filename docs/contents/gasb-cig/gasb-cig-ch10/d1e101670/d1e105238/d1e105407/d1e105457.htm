Chapter 10, Question 10.44.2

10.44.2.Q—A government enters into a pay-fixed, receive-variable interest rate swap, agreeing to pay an above-market fixed rate. The above-market rate allows the government to receive an up-front payment. How should the payment be reported in the basic financial statements?
A—Financial statements prepared using the economic resources measurement focus and the accrual basis of accounting (government-wide financial statements and proprietary and fiduciary funds) should report the up-front payment as an increase of debt. Financial statements using the current financial resources measurement focus and the modified accrual basis of accounting (governmental funds) should report the up-front payment as an other financing source.
