Chapter 6, Question 7.22.19

7.22.19Q—A government has unspent proceeds from a bond issue that are restricted for use in the acquisition and construction of capital assets. At the end of the fiscal year, the government reports a current liability for construction costs incurred but not yet paid as of the end of the fiscal year. Is the government required to classify the portion of the restricted assets that will be used to liquidate this current liability as a current asset?
A—No. Paragraph 31 of Statement 62 requires that resources designated for disbursement in the acquisition or construction of noncurrent assets be excluded from current assets; however, footnote 11 of Statement 62 provides that if such resources are considered to offset maturing debt that is properly reported as a current liability, those assets may be included within the current asset classification. At the end of the fiscal year, the government in this example already has acquired the noncurrent asset and recognized a current liability for the unpaid construction costs. Accordingly, the portion of the restricted assets may be, but is not required to be, classified as current.
