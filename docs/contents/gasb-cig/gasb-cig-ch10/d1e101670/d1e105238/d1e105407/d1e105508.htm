Chapter 10, Question 10.44.4

10.44.4.Q—A government enters into a swaption that is “in-the-money,” providing an up-front payment to the government. This instrument is a hybrid instrument composed of a borrowing and an at-the-market swaption. Shortly before the expiration of the swaption, it appears probable that the swaption will not be exercised. How should these events be reflected in the financial statements?
A—The borrowing should be reported consistent with the contingency guidance contained in Statement 62. That is, the expiration of the swaption, unexercised, is a gain contingency that should not be recognized prior to its realization. The probability of the swaption’s expiration, however, should be disclosed (paragraph 112 of Statement 62).
