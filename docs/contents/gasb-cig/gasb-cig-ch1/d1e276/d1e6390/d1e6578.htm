Chapter 1, Question 1.66.9

1.66.9.Q—If an entity discloses realized gains and losses, what additional disclosures are required?
A—Statement 31 generally prohibits the separate display of realized gains and losses on the face of the financial statements. However, it permits disclosure in the notes to the financial statements, provided the nature of those amounts and their relationship to the net change in the fair value of investments also is disclosed. That additional disclosure, which also is required if realized gains and losses are displayed in the separate reports of governmental external investment pools, is intended to inform financial statement users about the differences between measurements made based on the cost basis and those made on the fair value basis. Paragraph 15 of Statement 31 describes the required disclosures.
