Chapter 10, Question 10.5.1

10.5.1.Q—How is the leverage characteristic identified in derivative instruments?
A—Leverage occurs when a derivative instrument requires no initial net investment or a smaller initial net investment than other types of contracts that have a similar response to changes in market factors. For example, entering into a commodity futures contract generally requires no initial net investment, while purchasing the same commodity requires an initial net investment equal to its market price. Both contracts, however, will experience similar fair value gains and losses due to changes in the market price for the commodity.
