Chapter 3, Question 3.4.3

3.4.3.Q—If a government, such as a state, sponsors a cooperative group of primarily nongovernmental entities that have joined together to finance risk and risk is transferred from the participating entities, is the arrangement a public entity risk pool?
A—No. If a risk financing group consists primarily of nongovernmental participants and there is a transfer of risk from the participants to the group, then an insurance enterprise exists, not a pool. Insurance enterprises should be reported in an enterprise fund using the provisions of Statement 62, paragraphs 400–430.
