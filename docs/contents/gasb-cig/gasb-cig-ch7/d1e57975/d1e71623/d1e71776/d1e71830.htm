Chapter 7, Question 7.85.3

7.85.3.Q—Paragraph 113 of Statement 34 indicates that disclosures generally should focus on the primary government—specifically, its governmental activities, business-type activities, major funds, and nonmajor funds in the aggregate. Does that suggest that the detailed capital asset and long-term liability disclosures required by paragraph 116, as amended, should be made for each major enterprise fund and for nonmajor enterprise funds in the aggregate?
A—No. The focus of the disclosure requirements in paragraph 116, as amended, is the government-wide statement of net position. The disclosures need only identify major classes of assets and liabilities for governmental activities in total and for business-type activities in total. This specific requirement does not apply to the fund financial statements.
