Chapter Z, Question Z.51.5

Z.51.5.Q—Should water rights acquired by a utility enterprise fund with the intent that they be used in operations as the main source for water sold by the utility fund be considered an intangible asset that is acquired or created primarily for the purpose of directly obtaining income or profit and, therefore, be excluded from the scope of Statement 51 under paragraph 3a?
A—No. Any income or profit being generated by the utility fund through the sale of water is being generated from its overall operations, that is, providing basic government services to citizens. The water rights are being used in those operations and are not themselves directly generating the income or profit. Any income or profit associated with the use of the water rights is an indirect result of ownership of the rights. Therefore, the water rights would be an intangible asset subject to the provisions of Statement 51.
In contrast, if the utility fund acquired the water rights with the intention of generating income through trading those rights, instead of intending to use them in its operations, the water rights would be excluded from the scope of Statement 51 under paragraph 3a.
