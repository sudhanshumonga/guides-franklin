Chapter 9, Question 9.27.1

9.27.1.Q—Should a governmental entity that is financed primarily with nontax revenues use resident population to calculate the per capita debt ratios in the schedules of outstanding debt and general bonded debt?
A—Population is the standard data identified in paragraphs 24 and 26 of Statement 44 for calculating per capita ratios. For some governmental entities, especially those financed through user charges rather than from revenues provided by the general public, it may be more meaningful to divide outstanding debt by a measure of their customer or client base, such as customers for a utility or students for a public university.
