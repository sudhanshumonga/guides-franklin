Chapter 1, Question 1.32.1

1.32.1.Q—Deposits in some financial institutions are not federally insured; instead, they are covered by state insurance funds. Does such coverage allow the deposit to be considered insured?
A—Yes. Coverage by state insurance funds usually protects deposits from custodial credit risk. However, there have been limited instances of state insurance funds that were unable to cover insured losses. If the state insurance fund is not covering losses, the deposits should be reported as exposed to custodial credit risk.
