Chapter Z, Question Z.54.19

Z.54.19.Q—Paragraph 18 of Statement 54 provides guidance for governments that do not establish a policy for their use of unrestricted fund balance amounts. How should that provision be applied to determine the balances in the unrestricted fund balance classifications?
A—The guidance in paragraph 18 of Statement 54 provides that in situations where governments have not established a spending policy, the committed amounts would be reduced first, followed by assigned amounts, and then unassigned amounts when expenditures are incurred for purposes for which amounts in any of those classifications could be used. To determine the ending balances in each classification, the government should start with the balances that were committed and assigned at the beginning of the year, add amounts in the fund that have been committed or assigned during the year, then subtract expenditures made for the specific committed or assigned purposes. See nonauthoritative Appendix BZ-2.
