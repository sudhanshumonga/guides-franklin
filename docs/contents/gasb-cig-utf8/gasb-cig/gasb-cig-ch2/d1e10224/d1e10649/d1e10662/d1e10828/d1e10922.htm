Chapter 2, Question 2.13.4

2.13.4.Q—Does the issuance of warrants reduce cash?
A—Yes. The issuance of warrants, rather than the subsequent acceptance by the treasurer of the warrants, effectively reduces cash. That reduction of cash creates a cash flow. The substance, rather than the legal form, of the warrants should prevail. In a warrant system, participants draw down an available cash balance by issuing warrants. The system operates essentially as an internal cash management pool. It is irrelevant whether a warrant has been redeemed, just as it is irrelevant whether a check has been cashed, for determining cash balance. (See Question 2.11.3 for further explanation of cash flow timing.)
