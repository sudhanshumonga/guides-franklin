Chapter 7, Question 7.55.2

7.55.2.Q—Can governments use more than one column for nonmajor funds—for example, separate columns by fund type?
A—No. Paragraph 75 of Statement 34 states that nonmajor funds should be aggregated and presented in a single column. If a government wants to present a specific fund separately in the basic financial statements, even if it does not meet the percentage criteria, it should be reported as a major fund, rather than as a separate nonmajor fund.
