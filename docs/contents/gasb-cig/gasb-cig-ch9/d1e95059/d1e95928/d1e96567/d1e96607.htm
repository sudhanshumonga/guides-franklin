Chapter 9, Question 9.17.2

9.17.2.Q—If different tax rates are applied to different types of property, such as residential versus commercial, should the individual direct rates be shown in the revenue rate schedule?
A—Paragraph 16 of Statement 44 requires each individual direct rate applied by the reporting government to the revenue base to be presented. In general, this requires that each of the different rates applied to the different types of property should be shown for each of the last 10 years. Such differential rates for a particular levy should be labeled in a manner that distinguishes them from individual direct rates applied through separate levies. For example, the column headings for a government with three separate property tax levies, one of which applies differential rates, might look like the following:
General Levy
Residential
Commercial
Utility
Library Levy
Parks Levy
