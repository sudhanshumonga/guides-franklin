Chapter 1, Question 1.64.13

1.64.13.Q—In disclosing a portfolio's sensitivity to interest rate changes, a simulation model communicates the effects of volatile investments. In this case, would the additional disclosures for highly sensitive investments required by Statement 40, paragraph 15, be necessary?
A—The interest rate risk disclosure methods listed in paragraph 15 of Statement 40 range in sophistication from the simple to the complex. Some of the methods in paragraph 15 for disclosing interest rate risk may incorporate sufficient information required of highly sensitive investments so that additional disclosures may not be required. A disclosure that describes the effects of interest rate changes on aggregate portfolio values in both increasing and decreasing interest rate assumptions may include many, if not all, of the investment terms that paragraph 16 of Statement 40, as amended, would require to be disclosed. (See Question 1.63.3.) Specific additional disclosures that should be reported for highly sensitive investments when a simulation model is used to disclose interest rate risk have not been prescribed.
