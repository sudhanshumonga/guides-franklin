Chapter 9, Question 9.15.4

9.15.4.Q—Are governments required to present a ratio of assessed-value-to-estimated-actual-value-of-taxable-property?
A—No. Governments may include such a ratio if they believe it helps to meet the objectives of revenue base information and revenue capacity information in general. (For an example, see Exhibit I-8 in nonauthoritative Appendix B9-2.) If a government presents this ratio, it should indicate whether it is calculated as taxable-assessed-value or total-assessed-value-to-estimated-actual-value-of-taxable-property.
