Chapter 7, Question 7.21.9

7.21.9.Q—What is meant by "inexhaustible" collections or individual works of art or historical treasures?
A—Inexhaustible collections or individual works of art or historical treasures are those with extraordinarily long useful lives. Because of their cultural, aesthetic, or historical value, the holder of the asset (or assets) applies efforts to protect and preserve the asset in a manner greater than that for similar assets without such cultural, aesthetic, or historical value.
