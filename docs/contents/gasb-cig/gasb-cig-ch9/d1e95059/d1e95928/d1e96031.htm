Chapter 9, Question 9.12.5

9.12.5.Q—Is a government engaged only in fiduciary activities, such as a pension or OPEB plan, required to present any of the revenue capacity schedules?
A—No. Inflows for governments engaged only in fiduciary activities are additions, not revenues. However, governments may wish to voluntarily provide information in the statistical section related to the most significant sources of additions to its net position, if it believes the information will help to achieve the objectives of revenue capacity information. For example, a government might present a schedule of employer and employee contribution rates for the past 10 fiscal years; such a schedule might be analogous to revenue rate information. (See Exhibit H-3 in nonauthoritative Appendix B9-2, for example.) If such a government wanted to present information about investment income, which is a significant revenue source for many pension and OPEB plans, it might present information similar to that discussed in the answer to Question 9.14.3.
