Chapter 7, Question 7.23.4

7.23.4.Q—How is the "unspent portion" of capital debt proceeds determined?
A—The precision with which the unspent proceeds can be determined depends on the government's accounting records. Most governments are required to, or choose to, account for bond issues separately—either in separate funds or in an account or memorandum fashion in a multipurpose fund—and can identify what has been spent and what remains. Those governments whose accounting systems do not lend themselves to that type of specific tracking should use their best estimates—in a manner that can be documented—to determine the unspent portion.
