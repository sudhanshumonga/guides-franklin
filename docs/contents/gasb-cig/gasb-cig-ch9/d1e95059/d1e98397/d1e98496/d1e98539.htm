Chapter 9, Question 9.37.2

9.37.2.Q—How many operating and capital asset indicators should be presented for each function, program, or identifiable activity?
A—No specific number of indicators is required. A single indicator may be sufficient to convey to the reader the nature of the function or program, the types of activities it involves, the amount of activity conducted, and the capital assets available to support them. For broader functions, programs, or identifiable activities, two or more operating or capital asset indicators may be needed to convey that information.
