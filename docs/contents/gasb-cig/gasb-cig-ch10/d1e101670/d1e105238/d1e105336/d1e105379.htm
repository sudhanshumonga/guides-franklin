Chapter 10, Question 10.43.2

10.43.2.Q—A government enters into a forward contract to purchase a commodity. The forward contract, however, does not meet the criteria for the normal purchases and normal sales scope exception. The contract has an embedded cap with a reference rate based on the price of the commodity. Is the reference rate an inconsistent reference rate as provided in paragraph 64c(3) of Statement 53?
A—No. The economic characteristics and risks of the cap are closely related to the purchase contract, because the reference rate of the cap is the price of the commodity underlying the purchase contract. Hybrid instrument reporting, therefore, does not apply.
