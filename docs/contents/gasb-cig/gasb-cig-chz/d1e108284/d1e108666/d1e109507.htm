Chapter Z, Question Z.33.24

Z.33.24.Q—A government has a property tax rebate program that refunds a portion of property taxes paid, based on the taxpayer's income level. Should the rebates be reported as expenditures and expenses of a government program, or should they be reported as reductions of tax revenue?
A—Paragraphs 16 and 18 of Statement 33 require revenue from taxes to be recorded "net of estimated refunds," which includes rebates to taxpayers of amounts assessed. The rebates should be reported as reductions of property tax revenues of the period in which they become estimable.
