Chapter Z, Question Z.42.14

Z.42.14Q—Paragraph 17 of Statement 42 requires that impairment losses be reported as a program or operating expense, special item, or extraordinary item, as appropriate. Paragraph 21 requires that the impairment loss be reported net of the associated insurance recovery recognized in the year. If the insurance recovery is greater than the impairment loss, how should the net gain be reported?
A—For impairments that are special items or extraordinary items, the net gain would be reported in the same manner as a net loss. For impairment losses that would be considered program or operating expenses in the absence of any insurance recovery, the net gain should be reported as a program revenue (in the charges for services classification) or operating revenue, as appropriate. It would not be appropriate to report the net gain as a negative expense.
