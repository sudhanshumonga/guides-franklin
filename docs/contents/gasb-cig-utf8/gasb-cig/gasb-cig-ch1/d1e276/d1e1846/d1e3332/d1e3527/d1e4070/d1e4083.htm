Chapter 1, Question 1.34.1

1.34.1.Q—Can insurance or a surety bond provided by a commercial insurance company prevent deposits from being subject to custodial credit risk disclosures?
A—For a commercially insured deposit not to be exposed to custodial credit risk, the scope of coverage provided by a commercial insurance company would have to be substantially the same as that provided by federal deposit insurance. The same risks would have to be covered. That is, the insurance would have to cover all types of losses, not only certain types of losses (for example, only losses from theft).
