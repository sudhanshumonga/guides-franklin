Chapter Z, Question Z.48.15

Z.48.15Q—An enterprise fund pays the general fund to acquire a governmental activities’ capital asset at its fair value. The fair value of the asset is $300,000, and its carrying value in the government-wide statement of net position is $100,000. How should that transaction be reported in the fund financial statements?
A—Paragraph 15 of Statement 48 requires that assets transferred within the same reporting entity retain their carrying value. In this example, the enterprise fund should recognize a capital asset for $100,000 and a $200,000 transfer out to the general fund. The general fund should report an other financing source of $100,000 for the proceeds from the sale of a capital asset and a transfer in of $200,000. (See Question Z.48.4 about other intra-entity transfers of assets.)
