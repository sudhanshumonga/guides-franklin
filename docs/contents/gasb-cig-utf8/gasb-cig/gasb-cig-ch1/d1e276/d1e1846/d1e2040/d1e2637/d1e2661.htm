1.17.1.Q—How does one determine whether a security is or is not "held in the government's name"?
A—Holding in the government's name means that the custodial systems and records indicate the government's rights in the securities. The way in which securities are "held in the government's name" differs between investment securities and collateral and repurchase agreement securities. There also are differences between paper and book entry securities. Questions 1.18.1–1.25.1 discuss issues relating to this criterion in different circumstances in which the custodian is a party other than the counterparty.
