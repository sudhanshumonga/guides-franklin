Chapter 2, Question 2.23.2

2.23.2.Q—A state government authority received a cash payment in exchange for the future cash flows from the collections of significant delinquent accounts receivable. How should the cash payment received be reported in the statement of cash flows?
A—If the transaction meets the criteria in paragraph 6 of Statement No. 48, Sales and Pledges of Receivables and Future Revenues and Intra-Entity Transfers of Assets and Future Revenues, the receivables are considered to have been sold and the cash flows from that sale should be reported as cash flows from operations. If those criteria are not met, the transaction is considered to be a borrowing, and the cash flows would be classified in the noncapital or capital and related financing category, depending on the nature of the receivables.
