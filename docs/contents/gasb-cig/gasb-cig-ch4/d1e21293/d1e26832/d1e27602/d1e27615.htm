Chapter 4, Question 4.54.1

4.54.1.Q—What are jointly governed organizations?
A—Jointly governed organizations are organizations that provide goods or services to the citizenry of two or more governments, but that do not meet the definition of a joint venture because there is no ongoing financial interest or responsibility by the participating governments. For example, a regional utility authority created by a large number of local governments to operate electric power utilities for a multicounty area would be a jointly governed organization.
