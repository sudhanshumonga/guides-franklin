Chapter 6, Question 6.17.4

6.17.4.Q—Are flex repurchase agreements (flex repos) considered nonparticipating interest-earning investment contracts?
A—It depends on the terms of the flex repo. A flex repo is a term repurchase agreement that permits the investor to sell a portion of the collateral securities back to the counterparty before the final maturity date of the transaction. Flex repos frequently are used in construction projects when bond proceeds need to be invested until each portion of construction is required to be paid. A flex repo should be considered nonparticipating (and reported using cost-based measures) if it is neither negotiable nor transferable and if redemption terms do not consider market rates.
