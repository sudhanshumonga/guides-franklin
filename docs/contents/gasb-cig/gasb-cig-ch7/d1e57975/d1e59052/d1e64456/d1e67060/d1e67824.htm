Chapter 7, Question 7.47.24

7.47.24.Q—A city government is preparing its government-wide statements and is in the process of allocating the look-back adjustment for its internal service funds. A portion of the adjustment pertains to the enterprise fund operations. The answer to Question 7.47.7 states that the "crossover" amount should be reported as an internal balance in the government-wide statement of net position. Could the city instead report the crossover amount as a transfer in the statement of activities because the adjustment will not be made in the fund financial statements and will not be "repaid?"
A—No. To conclude that the amount representing an over- or under-charge by an internal service fund is a permanent transfer is contradictory to the principle that internal service funds should be operated on a break-even basis, with excesses or deficiencies eliminated within a reasonable time through adjustments to future internal charges. As the internal charges are adjusted over time, and as over- and under-charges fluctuate from year to year, the internal balance reported in the statement of net position will rise and fall but should not grow to a significant amount.
