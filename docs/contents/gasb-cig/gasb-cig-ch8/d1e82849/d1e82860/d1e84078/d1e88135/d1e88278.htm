Chapter 8, Question 8.42.8

8.42.8.Q—Technical Bulletin No. 2006-1, Accounting and Financial Reporting by Employers and OPEB Plans for Payments from the Federal Government Pursuant to the Retiree Drug Subsidy Provisions of Medicare Part D, requires that an employer measure and report the unfunded actuarial accrued liability for OPEB without reduction for projected future subsidy payments under the retiree drug subsidy option of Medicare Part D. May an employer include as additional information (a) what the unfunded actuarial accrued liability would be if projected future subsidy payments were netted in the measurement process and (b) the additional unfunded actuarial accrued liability estimated to be covered by the projected future subsidy payments?
A—Communication of the additional information in notes to the financial statements would not be inconsistent with the concepts discussed in Concepts Statement No. 3, Communication Methods in General Purpose External Financial Reports That Contain Basic Financial Statements, if an employer or OPEB plan concludes:
a.That the additional information "(a) is consistent with the objectives of state and local government financial reporting and (b) meets the basic characteristics for the communication of information in financial reports," (paragraph 26 of Concepts Statement 3) and, further,
b.That the additional information is consistent with the definition and criteria for notes to the financial statements discussed in paragraphs 35 and 36 of Concepts Statement 3—including that the information is "essential to a user's understanding of financial position or inflows and outflows of resources" of the employer.
The inclusion of additional information in the notes to the financial statements would not change or substitute for existing note disclosure requirements but would result in the disclosure of additional detail.
If it is concluded that the additional information would not be consistent with the preceding concepts regarding disclosure in notes to the financial statements, the employer may wish to consider whether such information would be consistent with the function of supporting information—that is, the information "places basic financial statements and notes to basic financial statements in an appropriate operational, economic, or historical context" (paragraph 40 of Concepts Statement 3). If so, the information may be presented as supplementary information, as the term is used in paragraph 46 of Concepts Statement 3. In no case should the additional information be presented in RSI, which is limited to information specified by the GASB.
Supplementary information, if presented, is not a substitute for information that is required to be displayed in basic financial statements, disclosed in notes to the financial statements, or presented as RSI.
