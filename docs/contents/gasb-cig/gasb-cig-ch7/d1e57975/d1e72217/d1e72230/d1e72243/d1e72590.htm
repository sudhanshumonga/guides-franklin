Chapter 7, Question 7.91.12

7.91.12.Q—If a government has a perspective difference between its budgetary practices and the GAAP financial reporting model, is it required to present its budgetary comparison as RSI?
A—Only in certain limited circumstances are governments required to present their budgetary comparisons as RSI. Statement No. 41, Budgetary Comparison Schedules—Perspective Differences, stipulates that the basic statement option is not available to governments with perspective differences that are so significant that they are not able to present budgetary comparisons for the general and major special revenue funds. Most perspective differences, as discussed in paragraphs 19–22 of NCGA Interpretation 10, do not render a government unable to present the required comparisons. However, as discussed in paragraph 8 in the Basis for Conclusions section of Statement 41, some governments have budgetary structures that prevent them from associating the estimated revenues and appropriations from their legally adopted budget to the major revenue sources and functional expenditures that they report in their general fund and major special revenue funds.
