Chapter 4, Question 4.44.1

4.44.1.Q—Paragraph 66 of Statement 14, as amended, provides that other stand-alone governments, such as joint ventures, jointly governed organizations, pools, and some special-purpose governments, should apply the provisions of Statement 14, as amended, "as if they were primary governments" when they issue separate financial statements. Does this mean that these governments are primary governments for financial reporting purposes?
A—No. Only those governments that meet the criteria in paragraph 13 are primary governments. However, a stand-alone government that does not meet the financial accountability criteria to be included as a component unit in the financial statements of a primary government should apply the provisions of Statement 14, as amended, "as if it were a primary government" for its financial reporting. For a stand-alone government, the financial reporting entity consists of the stand-alone government and any component units for which it is financially accountable.
