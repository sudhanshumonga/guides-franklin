Chapter 10, Question 10.6.1

10.6.1.Q—A government issues bonds 10
CQA, Chapter 10, Footnote 10—Unless otherwise indicated, the bonds that are referred to in the questions in this chapter are retired by a single payment.
with an embedded call option. Does the potential settlement of the government’s obligation to the creditor that would occur upon exercise of the call option meet the net settlement criterion in paragraph 13 of Statement 53?
A—Yes. The potential settlement of the government’s obligation that would occur upon the exercise of the option meets the net settlement criterion because it can readily be settled net outside the contract. (If the economic characteristics and risks of the embedded option and the bonds are not closely related, a hybrid instrument may be established. In many cases, however, the economic characteristics and risks of the embedded option and the bonds are closely related, and separate reporting of the embedded option and the bonds is not required. Questions 10.43.1 and 10.43.2 address what constitutes closely related.)
