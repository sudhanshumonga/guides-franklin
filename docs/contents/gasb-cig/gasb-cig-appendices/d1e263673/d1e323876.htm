Appendix B9-3: Statement 44, Paragraph 14—Calculation of the Total Direct Rate

This nonauthoritative appendix illustrates the calculation of the total direct rate as required by paragraph 14 of Statement 44. The total direct rate is the weighted average of all individual direct rates applied by a government to a particular revenue base.
Example A
A government has three property tax levies, all of which apply a single rate to all property—$1.00, $0.50, and $0.25 per $1,000 of assessed value. The calculation of the total direct rate would be a simple summation of the individual direct rates as follows:
$1.00 + $0.50 + $0.25 = $1.75
The direct rate for this government is $1.75.
Example B
A government sets differential rates for different types of property. One- and two-family homes are taxed at a rate of $1.00 per $1,000 of assessed value. residential property other than one- and two-family homes is taxed at a rate of $1.50 per $1,000 of assessed value. Real property other than residential property is taxed at a rate of $2.00 per $1,000 of assessed value. Becasue the government sets differential rates for different types of property, it will need to employ a formula for calculating a weighted average that multiplies each rate by the proportion of the revenue base to which it applies.
[rate x (amount of revenue base to which the rate applies + total taxable revenue base)]
The products from each calculation are summed to produce the total direct rate.
The direct rate for this government is $1.58.
