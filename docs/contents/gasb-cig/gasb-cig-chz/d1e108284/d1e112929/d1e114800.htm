Chapter Z, Question Z.54.60

Z.54.60.Q—A special revenue fund is used by a local government to account for a revenue that is restricted by state statute to be spent for a specific purpose. At year end, the entire fund balance is classified as restricted. Next year’s budget projects an excess of expenditures over revenues, causing the government to include a portion of existing fund balance as a budgetary resource. Should the amount necessary to balance the budget be classified as assigned?
A—No. The amount necessary to balance the budget is already classified as restricted to the purpose for which it is appropriated in the budget. Classifying a portion as assigned understates the restriction on the use of the resources. Paragraph 16 of Statement 54 provides that appropriating existing fund balance to remedy a budgetary imbalance “satisfies the criteria” to be classified as an assignment; however, that classification would only apply if the amount necessary to balance is expected to come from unassigned fund balance.
