Chapter 8, Question 8.1.3

8.1.3.Q—What is the relationship of Statement 45 to Statement 74, and how does a government determine which Statement or Statements apply?
A—Statement 45 applies to any employer that provides OPEB (that is, the employer pays all or part of the cost of the benefits, including implicit rate subsidies). It establishes the requirements for measurement and recognition of the employer's expense or expenditures and liabilities and for related disclosures and required supplementary information related to the employer's OPEB commitment.
Statement 74 applies to OPEB plans, whether included in a stand-alone plan financial report or in the financial report of another entity. It also applies to entities that hold assets accumulated for OPEB purposes in a fiduciary capacity and all employers that provide benefits through OPEB plans that are not administered through a trust that meets the criteria in paragraph 3 of the Statement. It establishes the requirements for reporting the responsible entity's stewardship of the assets that will be used to finance the payment of benefits as they come due.
An employer that provides OPEB is subject to the requirements of Statement 45. Whether Statement 74 also applies generally will depend on (a) whether the employer includes an OPEB plan as a trust or agency fund in its own financial report—if so, the employer should follow the applicable requirements of both Statement 45 (related to the employer's OPEB expense and liabilities) and Statement 74 (related to the trust or agency fund) and (b) whether the plan is administered through a trust that meets the criteria in paragraph 3 of Statement 74—if not, the employer should apply the requirements of paragraph 58 of Statement 74 for reporting of assets accumulated for OPEB purposes.
