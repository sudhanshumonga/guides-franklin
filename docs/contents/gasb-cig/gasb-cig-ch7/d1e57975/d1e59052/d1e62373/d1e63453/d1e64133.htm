Chapter 7, Question 7.24.23

7.24.23.Q—A state government passes enabling legislation that authorizes the raising of a gasoline tax and contains a legally enforceable restriction limiting the use of the revenues to street and highway projects. The legislation also requires the state to share a portion of the revenues with county governments. Should both the state and the counties disclose the revenues as net position restricted by enabling legislation?
A—Both the state and the counties would report the net position as restricted. However, only the state government would disclose the net position generated by the gasoline tax as restricted by enabling legislation. From the perspective of the counties, the net position is restricted by limitations imposed by the state, an outside party.
