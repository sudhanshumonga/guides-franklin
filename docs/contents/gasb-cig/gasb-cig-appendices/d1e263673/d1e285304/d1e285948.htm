Case 14—Sports and Recreation Commission
Facts: A sports and recreation commission was created as a separate legal entity by a metropolitan service district (a primary government). The sports and recreation commission is governed by a seven-member board appointed by the executive officer of the metropolitan service district. Through an intergovernmental agreement between the service district and the city, the commission is responsible for operation and management of three city-owned sports facilities, including all food service operations. The commission owns all of the food service equipment used in the facilities. Under the agreement, the city retains the title to the sports facilities and remains obligated to pay certain debt related to those facilities.
Conclusion: The commission and the city each have an undivided interest in the operation of the facilities. The city and the commission each hold title to the property that represents their interest in the operation of the facilities and each would report the assets, deferred outflows of resources, liabilities, deferred inflows of resources, expenditures/expenses, and revenues that are associated with their participation in the joint operation.
