Chapter 9, Question 9.32.1

9.32.1.Q—Is a government engaged only in fiduciary activities, such as a pension or OPEB plan, required to present any of the demographic and economic information schedules?
A—No. The required demographic and economic indicators (population, total personal income, per capita personal income, unemployment rate, and principal employers) generally would not be relevant for a fiduciary activity. Although there is no requirement to do so, such a government may present a schedule of demographic and economic information if it believes the information would be useful to its users and would help to achieve the objectives set forth in paragraph 6 of Statement 44. Pension and OPEB plans also might present demographic information about the members they cover, as do the illustrative retirement systems in Exhibits H-4 in nonauthoritative Appendix B9-2.
