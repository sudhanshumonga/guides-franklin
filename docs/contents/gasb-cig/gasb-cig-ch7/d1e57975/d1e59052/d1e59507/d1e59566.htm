Chapter 7, Question 7.9.2

7.9.2.Q—Are library books depreciable capital assets?
A—If library books are considered to have a useful life of greater than one year, they are capital assets and are depreciable. Because most library collections consist of a large number of books with modest values, group or composite depreciation methods (as discussed in paragraphs 163–166 of Statement 34) may be appropriate. (See Question 7.15.1.) In certain situations, library books may be considered works of art or historical treasures and could be reported using the provisions in paragraphs 27–29 of Statement 34.
