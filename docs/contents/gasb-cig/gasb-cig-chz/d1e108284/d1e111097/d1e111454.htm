Chapter Z, Question Z.49.6

Z.49.6.Q—Paragraph 19 of Statement 49 states that the measurement of a government's pollution remediation expense should be reduced by expected recoveries from other parties. Should liens that have been exercised on polluted property be considered expected recoveries from other parties?
A—Yes. The owners of polluted property typically are responsible parties for pollution remediation. Therefore, a lien that has been exercised is an expected recovery and its fair value should reduce the measurement of the pollution remediation expense. As discussed in paragraph 19, "if the expected recoveries are realized or realizable, they [also] should be recognized separately from the liability as recovery assets (for example, cash or receivables)." Liens typically are realizable and, therefore, would reduce remediation expense but not the liability.
