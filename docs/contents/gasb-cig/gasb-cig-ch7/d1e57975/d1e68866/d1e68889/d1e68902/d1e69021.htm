Chapter 7, Question 7.55.5

7.55.5.Q—Can a fiduciary fund that the government believes is particularly important to users be reported in a separate column like a major fund?
A—No. Fiduciary fund data should not be displayed separately as major funds. Paragraph 106 of Statement 34 requires a separate column to be presented for each fiduciary fund type (pension [and other employee benefit] trust funds, investment trust funds, private-purpose trust funds, and agency funds), and further addresses additional disclosure requirements for individual defined benefit pension plans and postemployment healthcare plans. In addition, similar separate disclosure requirements for each individual investment trust fund is required by paragraph 19 of Statement 31, as amended. Governments may present combining statements for the fiduciary fund types as supplementary information if they want to provide financial statements for individual funds.
