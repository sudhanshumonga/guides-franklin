Chapter 7, Question 7.24.25

7.24.25.Q—A government issues bonds for capital construction but is required by the bond indenture to establish a bond and interest reserve from the proceeds. The cash and investments in the bond and interest reserve are included in the calculation of restricted net position. Should the portion of the debt attributable to the establishment of the reserve be included in the calculation of the net investment in capital assets or restricted component of net position?
A—The restricted component. Because the resources initially deposited into the reserve account represent proceeds of debt that were not "attributable to the acquisition, construction, or improvement" of capital assets, that portion of the debt should be included in the calculation of restricted net position. The resources in the reserve constitute unspent proceeds. If there are accumulated earnings from investing the resources in the reserve account, they should be included in the calculation of either restricted net position or unrestricted net position, as appropriate. (See also Questions 7.23.2 and 7.23.4 about treatment of unspent proceeds and Question 7.24.7 about liabilities related to restricted assets.)
