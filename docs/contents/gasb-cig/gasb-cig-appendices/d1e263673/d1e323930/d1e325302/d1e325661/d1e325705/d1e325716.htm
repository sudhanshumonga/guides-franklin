Chapter 10—Appendix 2, Illustration 2.2

On July 1, 20X0, a city issues variable-rate demand bonds of $100 million. The bonds mature on June 30, 20X4. The coupon rate is remarketed weekly. At the same time, the city enters into a $100 million notional, pay-fixed, receive-variable interest rate swap. The variable rate of the semiannual payment also resets weekly. The variable rate is 49.96 percent of the London Interbank Offered Rate (LIBOR) plus 78 basis points. The fixed rate is 3.57872 percent and the swap terminates on June 30, 20X4. Upon association with the variable-rate bonds (at the inception of the swap), the fair value of the swap is zero. Interest rates fluctuate after the swap is executed. The city continues to pay the contractual fixed rate (3.57872 percent) to the counterparty. The actual synthetic rate, however, varies depending on the difference between the city’s remarketed rate paid to bondholders and the swap’s variable payment made by the counterparty to the city.
