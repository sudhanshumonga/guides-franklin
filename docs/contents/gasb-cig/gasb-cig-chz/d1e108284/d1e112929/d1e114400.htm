Chapter Z, Question Z.54.44

Z.54.44.Q—Is a debt service fund required to be used for all debt service activity?
A—No. A debt service fund is required to be used when it is legally mandated or to account for resources that are being accumulated for principal and interest maturing in future years. The accumulation of resources is distinguished from the annual receipt and collection of resources that will be used to make the next principal and interest payments that come due. Setting aside resources in a bond and interest reserve account or establishing a sinking fund are examples of accumulating resources for future years' debt service.
