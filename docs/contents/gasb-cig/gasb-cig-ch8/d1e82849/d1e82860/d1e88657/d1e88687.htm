Chapter 8, Question 8.46.2

8.46.2.Q—If one employer (for example, a state) in a cost-sharing defined benefit retiree healthcare plan (one that meets the requirements of paragraph 22a of Statement 45) is legally responsible for all contractually required contributions, including contributions on behalf of other employers in the plan (for example, school districts), how should the plan be described in financial reports of the employers and the plan?
A—All participating employers (the state and the school districts) and the plan should identify the plan as a cost-sharing plan with a special funding situation, as discussed in paragraph 32 of Statement 45.
