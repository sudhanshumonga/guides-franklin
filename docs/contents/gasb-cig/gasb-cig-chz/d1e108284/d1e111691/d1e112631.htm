Chapter Z, Question Z.51.26

Z.51.26.Q—Should a government begin to amortize an intangible asset if it is no longer considered to have an indefinite useful life?
A—Yes. Paragraph 17 of Statement 51 states that if changes in factors and conditions result in the useful life of an intangible asset no longer being indefinite, the asset should begin to be amortized. However, the asset first should be tested for impairment in accordance with the guidance in Statement 42 because a change in the expected duration of use of the intangible asset has occurred. The remaining carrying value of the intangible asset, if any, following the recognition of any impairment loss should be amortized in subsequent reporting periods over the remaining estimated useful life of the asset. This change should be accounted for as a change in accounting estimate similar to a change in the useful life of any type of capital asset.
