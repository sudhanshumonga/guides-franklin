Chapter 8, Question 8.45.2

8.45.2.Q—If an OPEB plan has defined contribution characteristics but also provides a defined benefit in some form, should the employer apply the requirements of Statement 45 applicable to defined contribution plans or those applicable to defined benefit plans?
A—Regardless of whether defined contribution features are present, if an OPEB plan provides a benefit that is a function of factors other than the amounts contributed to an active member's account during employment and amounts earned on contributed assets, the employer should apply the requirements of Statement 45 applicable to defined benefit plans.
For example, a plan that directs an employer to contribute a specified amount to each plan member’s account each month, during both active service and retirement, and specifies that the contributions and earnings on the contributions can only be used for postemployment healthcare would not be considered a defined contribution plan. Although the benefit terms provide for an individual account for each plan member and define contributions to be made by the employer during the member’s periods of active service, employer contributions to the account also are required after the plan member’s active service period. Thus, the employer should apply the requirements of Statement 45, as amended, applicable to defined benefit plans.
