Chapter Z, Question Z.42.12

Z.42.12.Q—Paragraph 17 of Statement 42, as amended, provides guidance on how the loss from the impairment of a capital asset should be reported. Which capital asset accounts should be adjusted when the loss is reported?
A—The effect of an impairment can be included in the capital asset accounts in either of two ways. First, the accumulated depreciation account may be increased by the amount of the loss. Second, both the capital asset historical cost account and related accumulated depreciation account could be decreased proportionately such that the net decrease equals the impairment loss. In the former circumstance, the impairment is treated as if additional years of useful life of the capital asset had been used up through the impairment. In the latter circumstance, the impairment is treated like a disposal of a portion of the capital asset.
