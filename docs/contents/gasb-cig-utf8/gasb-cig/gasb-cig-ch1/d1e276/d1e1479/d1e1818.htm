Chapter 1, Question 1.9.13

1.9.13.Q—If a bond mutual fund is unrated but substantially all of the underlying securities within the fund are rated, should the government disclose the average rating of the underlying investments?
A—A mutual fund may not be rated by NRSROs for various reasons, including the inability to rate certain of the holdings within the mutual fund portfolio. Statement 40, paragraph 7, as amended, requires that the government disclose the fact that the mutual fund investment is not rated.
