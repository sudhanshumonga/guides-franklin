Chapter Z, Question Z.51.38

Z.51.38Q—A governmental entity that utilizes cloud computing services may incur costs related to the preparation and configuration of the existing system to be able to utilize those services. Would related software costs that otherwise meet the criteria for capitalization in Statement 51, be subject to capitalization even though they relate to modification made in association with a service fee arrangement?
A—Yes. External modifications of computer software should be capitalized if they result in an increase in functionality or efficiency of the computer software or an extension of the estimated useful life of the software. Therefore, conceptually, the outlays of the service fee arrangement should be allocated between upgrades that result in one of the aforementioned outcomes (capitalizable) and all other maintenance provided by the vendor (expensed as incurred).
