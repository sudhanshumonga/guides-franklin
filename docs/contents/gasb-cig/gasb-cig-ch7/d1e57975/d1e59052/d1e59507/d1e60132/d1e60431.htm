Chapter 7, Question 7.12.12

7.12.12.Q—When infrastructure assets are sold or otherwise disposed of in a transaction that does not constitute a disposal of government operations within the scope of Statement No. 69, Government Combinations and Disposals of Government Operations, how is a gain or loss calculated for the statement of activities?
A—A gain or loss would be calculated in the same manner as for other capital assets. The net book value of the infrastructure asset would be subtracted from the net amount realized from the sale or disposal. If the infrastructure asset was being depreciated, its net book value would be its historical cost or estimated historical cost, less accumulated depreciation. If the modified approach was being used, its net book value would be historical cost or estimated historical cost. (See Question 7.15.2 about removing assets that were depreciated using a composite method.)
