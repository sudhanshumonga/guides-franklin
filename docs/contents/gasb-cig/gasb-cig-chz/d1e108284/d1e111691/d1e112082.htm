Chapter Z, Question Z.51.7

Z.51.7.Q—Should an intangible asset acquired or created by a government with the intent to resell it be accounted for under the provisions of Statement 51?
A—No. An intangible asset acquired or created with the intent that it be resold generally is acquired or created primarily for the purpose of directly obtaining income or profit. Therefore, these assets would not be subject to the provisions of Statement 51 based on the scope exclusion in paragraph 3a of the Statement.
